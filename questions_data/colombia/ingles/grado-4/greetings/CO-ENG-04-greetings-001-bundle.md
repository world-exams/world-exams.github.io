## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-04-greetings-001-v1`

### Contexto
Waking up in the morning.

### Enunciado
What do you say to your teacher when you arrive at school at 7:00 AM?

### Options
- [x] A) Good morning
- [ ] B) Good night
- [ ] C) Good afternoon
- [ ] D) Goodbye

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Good morning" (buenos días) is used from sunrise until noon.

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-04-greetings-001-v8`

### Contexto
Time of day greetings.
It is **3:00 PM** and you see your neighbor.

### Enunciado
What is the best greeting for this time?

### Options
- [x] A) Good afternoon
- [ ] B) Good morning
- [ ] C) Good night
- [ ] D) Hello morning

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Good afternoon" (buenas tardes) is used from noon until early evening (around 5:00-6:00 PM).

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-04-greetings-001-v9`

### Contexto
Social etiquette at night.
You are leaving a party at 9:00 PM to go to sleep.

### Enunciado
What is the correct way to say goodbye at night before sleeping?

### Options
- [x] A) Good night
- [ ] B) Good evening
- [ ] C) Good morning
- [ ] D) Hello

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Good night" (feliz noche / que descanses) is used specifically when leaving or going to bed at night. "Good evening" is for arriving.
