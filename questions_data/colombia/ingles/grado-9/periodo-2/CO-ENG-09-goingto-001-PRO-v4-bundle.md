# Bundle: Plans & Purpose

This bundle explores the use of "be going to" for expressing pre-decided plans and intentions. Students will model career paths, community projects, and personal resolutions, moving from direct grammar application to complex ethical analysis of intentionality.

---

## Question 1 (Leisure Planning - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-goingto-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramática (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
*Conversation:*
John: What are your plans for the weekend?
Sarah: I _______ my grandparents in the countryside.

### Options
- [x] A) am going to visit
- [ ] B) will visit
- [ ] C) am visiting to
- [ ] D) go to visit

### Explicación Pedagógica
Se usa "be going to" para expresar un plan o intención que ya ha sido decidido antes del momento de hablar.

## Question 10 (Analysis of Motivation - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-goingto-001-PRO-v10`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comprensión Inferencial (Parte 6)
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
"Maria is going to resign from her high-paying job. She intends to move to a small village and open a sustainable library for children."

### Enunciado
What can we infer about Maria's intentions? (Select all that apply)

### Options
- [x] A) She prioritizes personal values over financial gain. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Her future plan involves a significant lifestyle change. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) She hates books and teaching children.
- [x] D) She has already decided to leave her current career. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] E) She is going to move to the capital city.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Dejar un trabajo bien pagado por una biblioteca en un pueblo implica priorizar valores (A) y cambiar de vida (B). Usar "is going to resign" implica una decisión tomada (D).

## Question 11 (Prediction vs Intention - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-goingto-001-PRO-v11`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramática (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Look at those dark clouds! It _______ (1) rain soon. I _______ (2) take my umbrella.

### Options
- [x] A) is going to / am going to
- [ ] B) will / will
- [ ] C) is going to / will
- [ ] D) will / am going to

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Is going to" se usa para predicciones basadas en evidencia presente (nubes negras) y para planes/intenciones decididos (llevar el paraguas). *Nota: También se acepta 'will' para decisiones espontáneas, pero en este contexto de plan de salida 'going to' es fuerte.*

## Question 20 (Transfer & Paradox - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-goingto-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Todas (Integración)
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
"If everyone in the world is going to do exactly what they intend at the same time, would the world become a perfect order or a chaotic collision of conflicting plans?"

### Enunciado
What does this thought experiment reveal about the nature of "intentions" (going to)?

### Options
- [x] A) That individual plans are interdependent; your "is going to" often relies on others being "not going to", highlighting the systemic limit of personal agency. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) That everyone should follow the same plan to avoid traffic. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [x] C) That grammar is the only thing that prevents the world from collapsing. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [ ] D) That intentions don't matter because the future is random. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is a philosophical synthesis of social systems and personal intent.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Pregunta de nivel extraordinario sobre la interdependencia de los sistemas y la limitación de la voluntad individual.
