# Bundle: The Future We Forecast

This bundle focuses on expressing predictions and uncertainties about the future using "will" and "won't". It covers contexts ranging from simple weather forecasts to complex sociological and technological predictions, aligning with ICFES Parts 1-7 throughout the period.

---

## Question 1 (Environmental Prediction - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramática (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
Scientists believe that if we don't reduce plastic waste, there _______ more plastic than fish in the oceans by 2050.

### Options
- [x] A) will be
- [ ] B) going to be
- [ ] C) are being
- [ ] D) won't have

### Explicación Pedagógica
Se usa "will" para una predicción basada en una creencia o proyección científica general sobre el futuro distante.

## Question 10 (Analysis of Intent - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v10`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comprensión Inferencial (Parte 6)
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
"Smart cities will likely prioritize pedestrian safety over car speed, implementating AI-driven traffic systems that will drastically reduce accidents."

### Enunciado
Select ALL valid inferences based on the prediction. (Credit: partial)

### Options
- [x] A) The priority of urban planning will shift towards people instead of vehicles. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Technology will play a crucial role in lowering mortality rates in cities. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) Cars will be completely banned from smart cities in the future.
- [x] D) Traffic lights will probably no longer be controlled manually. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] E) Pedestrians won't have to follow any traffic rules in smart cities.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.

### Explicación Pedagógica
El texto menciona "prioritize pedestrian safety" (A), "reduce accidents" (B) y "AI-driven systems" (D). C y E son inferencias extremas no soportadas.

## Question 11 (Prediction vs Plan - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v11`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramática (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Listen to this conversation:
Officer: "What are your plans for the new park?"
Mayor: "We are going to build a fountain, and I'm sure the citizens _______ it."

### Enunciado
Why did the Mayor use "are going to" and then "will"?

### Options
- [x] A) "Are going to" expresses a fixed plan, while "will" expresses a confident prediction about a reaction.
- [ ] B) Both mean exactly the same; there is no grammatical difference.
- [ ] C) "Will" is used only for things that are 100% certain to happen.
- [ ] D) The Mayor made a mistake; he should have used "will" for both.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Es un punto crítico de B1: usar "going to" para planes (intenciones) y "will" para predicciones/reacciones esperadas.

## Question 16 (Interdisciplinary Logic - Difficulty 6)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v16`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Análisis Crítico (Parte 6)
**Bloom:** Analyze+

### Contexto
In economics, the "Luddite Fallacy" suggests that new technology will destroy jobs. However, history shows it will simply reallocate labor to new sectors.

### Enunciado
Select ALL conclusions that align with the text's prediction. (Credit: partial)

### Options
- [x] A) Technological progress will shift the nature of work rather than end it. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Unemployment will not necessarily be a permanent global result of automation. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) Machines will eventually perform 100% of all human tasks by 2030.
- [x] D) Workers will need to learn new skills to adapt to future market demands. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] E) Technology will always be harmful to low-income populations.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.

### Explicación Pedagógica
El texto dice "reallocate labor" (A/B) y esto implica adaptación (D). El texto no apoya C ni E.

## Question 18 (Causal Logic - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comprensión Crítica (Parte 6)
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
*Context from Biology/Physics:* "As sea levels rise, the salinity will compromise coastal aquifers, which will eventually trigger mass migrations."

### Enunciado
Which chain of events is accurately predicted by the text?

### Options
- [x] A) Warming -> Ice melting -> Sea level rise -> Saltwater in wells -> Migration. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Rising water -> Migration -> Water pollution -> Salinity. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [x] C) Environmental changes will lead to socio-economic displacements. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] D) Migrations will cause the salt to enter the water. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the most detailed causal path (1.0).
- C is a correct high-level summary (0.8).

### Explicación Pedagógica
El estudiante debe reconstruir la cadena causal implícita en la predicción "will-will-will".

## Question 19 (Meta-Cognitive Reading - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Análisis de Sesgo (Parte 6)
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
"Data suggests that the gap between rich and poor nations will widen in the 'Silicon Era', as automation will favor owners of capital over holders of labor."

### Enunciado
Why did the author use "automation will favor owners" instead of "automation might favor"?

### Options
- [x] A) To present a deterministic economic theory as an inevitable certainty to provoke a reaction. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Because the author thinks it's a happy and positive event for everyone. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->
- [x] C) To sound more academic and professional by using strong modal verbs. <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [ ] D) To hide the fact that they don't have any real data on the subject. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A identifies the rhetorical use of "will" for determinism (1.0).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 9: Analizar el *por qué* se elige un modal de certeza absoluta ("will") en un tema sociopolítico complejo.

## Question 20 (Transfer & Synthesis - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-will-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Todas (Integración)
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
"In the quantum future, the concept of a singular 'will happen' will collide with the concept of 'many things happening at once', potentially breaking the linear way we predict time."

### Enunciado
How does this prediction challenge the standard grammatical use of "will"?

### Options
- [x] A) It suggests that "will" refers to a probability cloud rather than a fixed linear destination, shifting the language from certainty to superposition. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) It says that "will" is only for scientists and not for normal students. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [x] C) It proves that grammar is more important than physics in the future. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) It doesn't challenge it; the sentence is grammatically incorrect. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is a master-level transfer of physics to linguistic theory (1.0).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta cierra el bundle invitando a cuestionar la raíz del tiempo futuro ("will") desde una perspectiva de física teórica.
