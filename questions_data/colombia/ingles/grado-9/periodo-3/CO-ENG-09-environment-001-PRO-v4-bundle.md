# Bundle: Environment Vocabulary (ICFES Part 4)

This bundle focuses on environmental vocabulary and grammar in context (cloze tests), progressing from basic ecological terms to complex abstract debates on climate policy, sustainability, and human impact.

---

## Question 1 (Basic Ecosystems - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-environment-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Lexical (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
Read the text and choose the correct word for the blank.
"The Amazon is the world's largest tropical rainforest. It is home to millions of plant and animal _________."

### Enunciado
Select the best word to complete the text.

### Options
- [x] A) species
- [ ] B) spaces
- [ ] C) spices
- [ ] D) slices

### Explicación Pedagógica
La palabra correcta es "species" (especies). "Spaces" (espacios) no tiene sentido biológico, y "spices" (especias) es un distractor fonético.

## Question 18 (Mitigation vs Adaptation Strategies - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-environment-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Pragmática/Lexical
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
In climate change policy, leaders must allocate budgets between "mitigation" (stopping the root cause) and "adaptation" (surviving the effects).

### Enunciado
Which policy represents a pure "adaptation" strategy?

### Options
- [x] A) Building massive sea walls to protect coastal cities from rising tides. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Developing drought-resistant genetically modified crops. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [x] C) Restoring mangrove forests to act as natural storm barriers. <!-- weight: 0.5 -->
- [ ] D) Transitioning the national energy grid entirely to solar power. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is pure adaptation (defensive engineering). B is biological adaptation. C is adaptation with some mitigation benefits. D is pure mitigation (stopping emissions).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 8: Diferenciación conceptual estricta utilizando léxico técnico de políticas públicas (mitigación vs. adaptación). D lucha contra la causa; A lucha contra el efecto.

## Question 19 (Meta-linguistic Framing of Nature - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-environment-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Lexico-Gramatical (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
Linguists argue that how we refer to nature affects how we treat it. Calling a forest "timber resources" rather than "a complex ecosystem" shifts the cognitive frame.

### Enunciado
Select the phrasing that most profoundly reduces nature to a purely utilitarian commodity.

### Options
- [x] A) Extractable ecological capital waiting for harvest. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Natural raw materials for industrial production. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [x] C) The environment surrounding our factories. <!-- weight: 0.3 -->
- [ ] D) The sacred web of life that sustains us all. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A utilitiza el lenguaje del capitalismo financiero extremo ("capital", "extractable", "harvest").

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 9: Análisis semántico del framing ecológico. Reconocer cómo el vocabulario económico convierte a la naturaleza en "mercancía extractiva".

## Question 20 (Deep Ecology & Cosmic Transfer - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-environment-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Lexico-Gramatical
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
Deep ecology suggests that human life has no greater inherent value than any other life form. If this principle were applied to terraforming Mars (altering an alien planet to suit human life)...

### Enunciado
How would a strict adherent to deep ecology describe the terraforming of a lifeless rock like Mars?

### Options
- [x] A) As an unjustified hubristic expansion that projects Earth's biological imperialism onto an aesthetically ancient, undisturbed realm. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) As a moral duty to spread the gift of consciousness to a dead planet. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->
- [x] C) As an act of geological vandalism, destroying the innate, non-biological value of Martian topography. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] D) As an expensive project that will harm the Earth's economy. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->

### Scoring
- A and C correctly apply "inherent value" to non-human/non-biological entities, condemning expansion.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 10: Transferir la ética de la "ecología profunda" a un escenario de astrofísica y ética planetaria (terraforomo), cuestionando el antropocentrismo imperante.
