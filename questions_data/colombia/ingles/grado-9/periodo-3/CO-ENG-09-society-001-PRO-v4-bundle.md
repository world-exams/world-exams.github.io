# Bundle: Society Vocabulary (ICFES Part 4)

This bundle explores social vocabulary in contextual cloze texts. It starts with basic community roles and civic duties, progressing towards complex sociological phenomena such as systemic bias, institutional logic, and the philosophy of social contracts.

---

## Question 1 (Community Roles - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Lexical (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
Read the text and choose the correct word for the blank.
"In a healthy community, neighbors help each other and work together as a _________ to solve local problems."

### Enunciado
Select the best word to complete the text.

### Options
- [x] A) team
- [ ] B) crowd
- [ ] C) fight
- [ ] D) game

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Team" (equipo) es la única palabra que encaja con "work together" (trabajar juntos) y "solve problems" (resolver problemas).

**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
Intersectionality is an analytical framework for understanding how aspects of a person's social and political identities combine to create different modes of discrimination and privilege.

### Enunciado
Which scenario is the MOST pristine application of intersectional analysis?

### Options

## Question 19 (Meta-linguistic Framing of Poverty - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Sociolingüística (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
Policy documents often use the passive voice to describe societal failures, obscuring moral responsibility. Compare these semantic choices describing a neighborhood's economic collapse.

### Enunciado
Select the phrasing that most actively assigns blame to systemic/institutional actors, rather than treating poverty as a natural phenomenon.

### Options
- [x] A) Corporate redlining and systemic disinvestment systematically deprived the neighborhood of resources. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The neighborhood suffered a tragic decline in wealth and opportunities over the decades. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->
- [x] C) A wave of economic hardship inexplicably swept through the community, leaving poverty in its wake. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [ ] D) The residents failed to adapt to the changing modern macroeconomic conditions. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A asigna culpa explícita a agentes (corporate redlining = práctica racista de bancos). B y C tratan a la pobreza como el clima ("swept through", "tragic decline"). D culpa a las propias víctimas.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 9: Análisis semiótico de políticas. El estudiante debe distinguir entre escritura en "voz pasiva u oscurecedora" y la exposición directa de los agentes sistémicos ("instituciones" haciéndolo de forma activa).

## Question 20 (Social Construct Constructivism - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Sociolingüística
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
Sociology teaches that money and borders are "social constructs" (they exist only because a collective believes in them). An alien species that only perceives absolute physical truth lands on Earth.

### Enunciado
How would this alien describe a human "bank" if it cannot comprehend social constructs?

### Options
- [x] A) A highly fortified building protecting small pieces of printed fiber and digital servers with glowing lights, holding no biological sustenance. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) A central hub of economic power where the most important assets of the society are guarded securely. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->
- [x] C) A sanctuary where humans trade hours of their biological existence for symbols printed on cloth. <!-- weight: 0.6 -->
- [ ] D) A place where humans go to get happy when they have worked hard. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the ultimate "social construct stripping" response (looking purely at physical ontology). C blends physical reality with the beginnings of social interpretation. B completely falls into the social construct illusion.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 10: Un salto mental "Extraordinario". Para entender la ontología de un "Constructo Social", debes ser capaz de deconstruirlo por completo y describirlo solo a través de la realidad física pura.
