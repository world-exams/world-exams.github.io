# Bundle: Prepositional Phrases (ICFES Parts 4 & 7)

This bundle focuses on grammatical cloze tests involving prepositions. It progresses from basic spatial and temporal prepositions to complex dependent prepositions (adjective/verb + preposition) and abstract metaphorical uses in advanced discourse.

**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto

### Enunciado
Select the correct preposition.

### Options
- [x] A) on
- [ ] B) in
- [ ] C) at
- [ ] D) of

### Explicación Pedagógica
Se utiliza la preposición "on" para días de la semana y fechas específicas (on Friday, on Monday morning).

## Question 9 (Movement: Through vs Across - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v9`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical (Parte 4)
**Bloom:** Analyze

### Contexto
"To get to the hidden waterfall, we had to walk _________ a dense, dark forest filled with wild animals."

### Enunciado
Select the correct preposition of movement.

### Options
- [x] A) through
- [ ] B) across
- [ ] C) over
- [ ] D) along

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 10 (Advanced Dependent Prepositions - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v10`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Lexico-Gramatical (Parte 7)
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Academic writing requires precise pairings of verbs and prepositions.

### Enunciado
Select ALL sentences that use the CORRECT dependent preposition.

### Options
- [x] A) She successfully recovered from the illness after a week in the hospital. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The author entirely relies on secondary sources for his research. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) The manager finally agreed with the new budget proposal.
- [x] D) They objected to the strict regulations imposed by the committee. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] E) The teenager always insisted to go to the party alone.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 5: "Recover from" (D), "rely on" (A), "object to" (D) son correctas. Errores: C (agreed *to* a proposal, not with), E (insisted *on* going, not to).

## Question 16 (Spatial Metaphors in Business - Difficulty 6)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v16`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Lexico-Gramatical (Parte 7)
**Bloom:** Analyze+

### Contexto
English frequently uses prepositions of physical movement (up, down, across) as metaphors for abstract business and economic concepts.

### Enunciado
Select ALL sentences where the preposition is used correctly in an abstract, metaphorical sense.

### Options
- [x] A) The stock market rebounded and went through the roof after the positive employment report. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Small businesses are struggling to stay afloat under the weight of new tax regulations. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) Corporate profits flew over the window when the scandal broke out.
- [x] D) The startup managed to get its innovative project off the ground despite early setbacks. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] E) The CEO walked across the floor when sales dropped.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 6: Comprensión de metáforas espaciales idiomáticas. "Through the roof" (crecimiento explosivo), "stay afloat" (sobrevivir económicamente), "off the ground" (iniciar exitosamente). C y E usan mal las preposiciones metafóricas ("flew out the window", no "over").

## Question 18 (C2 Dependent Collocations - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Lexico-Gramatical (Parte 7)
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
Extreme precision is required in academic English when attributing qualities to objects or ideas.

### Enunciado
Which sentence uses the most accurate and formal dependent preposition collocating with the adjective?

### Options
- [x] A) The philosopher's arguments are entirely devoid of empirical evidence. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The local government is lacking in basic resources to fight the pandemic. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [x] C) The community was completely deprived from access to clean water. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) The new theory is independent from any religious doctrine. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is perfect formal C2 English ("devoid of"). B is correct but slightly less formal. C is grammatically incorrect (should be "deprived OF"). D is grammatically incorrect (should be "independent OF").

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 8: Nivel C2 de "Dependent prepositions". Identificar que "devoid" usa "of", mientras que "deprived" e "independent" también requieren "of" de manera estricta en el uso formal, no "from".

## Question 19 (Prepositional Ambiguity in Law - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Pragmática/Lexical
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
A legal contract reads: "The tenant is responsible for damages IN the apartment BY the tenant." A lawyer argues this means damages caused *outside* the literal walls (e.g., the balcony) aren't covered.

### Enunciado
Select the prepositional phrase that a legal drafter should use to unequivocally cover the apartment, its fixtures, structure, and surrounding assigned property.

### Options
- [x] A) Damages to, within, or arising from the utilization of the leased premises. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Damages onto the apartment and its outside zones. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [x] C) Damages inside and around the walls of the apartment. <!-- weight: 0.5 -->
- [ ] D) Damages at the apartment location by people. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A uses precise multi-prepositional legal phrasing ("to" for structural harm, "within" for interior, "arising from" for causation).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 9: Metacognición sobre cómo las preposiciones determinan la responsabilidad legal. "In" es demasiado limitante espacialmente; la opción A usa una triada preposicional (to, within, from) para cerrar vacíos legales (loopholes).

## Question 20 (Ontological Prepositions: Being vs Having - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-prepositions-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Sociolingüística (Parte 7)
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
Phenomenologists argue that the English language biases us toward seeing the world as objects we possess rather than events we participate in, specifically through prepositional choices.

### Enunciado
Which prepositional framing best represents a shift from a "philosophy of possession/objects" back to a "philosophy of state/being"?

### Options
- [x] A) Saying "I am in sorrow" rather than "I have sorrow." <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Saying "The tree belongs to the forest" rather than "The forest has trees." <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [x] C) Saying "I suffer from a cold" rather than "I caught a cold." <!-- weight: 0.6 -->
- [ ] D) Saying "My car is fast" rather than "The fastness of my car." <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A shifts the human subject from an owner who literally "possesses" an emotion (have), to an entity located *within* a state of experience ("in").

### Explicación Pedagógica
Nivel 10: Transferencia filosófica. Analizar cómo preposiciones espaciales simples ("in") pueden usarse ontológicamente para reencuadrar la experiencia humana como un estado envolvente en lugar de un objeto adquirido ("have").
