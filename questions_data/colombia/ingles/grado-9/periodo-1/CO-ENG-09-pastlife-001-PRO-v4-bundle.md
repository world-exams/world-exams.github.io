# Bundle: Stories of the Past

This bundle focuses on the Past Simple tense, historical context, and the structure of biographies, moving from basic facts to complex historical analysis and cross-generational synthesis.

---

## Question 1 (Past Verbs - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-pastlife-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Lexical
**Bloom:** Remember

### Contexto
Yesterday, I ________ (eat) an apple for lunch.

### Enunciado
What is the correct past form of the verb "eat"?

### Options
- [x] A) ate
- [ ] B) eated
- [ ] C) eating
- [ ] D) eats

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Ate" es la forma irregular correcta del pasado simple del verbo "eat". Las otras opciones son incorrectas (eated no existe; eating es gerundio; eats es presente).

## Question 2 (Historical Context - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-pastlife-001-PRO-v2`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Lexical
**Bloom:** Remember

### Contexto
In the year 1800, people ________ horses to travel.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb for this historical routine:

### Options
- [x] A) rode
- [ ] B) drive
- [ ] C) fly
- [ ] D) sail

### Explicación Pedagógica
El verbo "ride" (pasado: "rode") se utiliza para caballos. "Drive" es para carros, "fly" para aviones y "sail" para barcos, los cuales no eran el medio de transporte terrestre común en 1800.

## Question 16 (Historical Analysis - Difficulty 6)

**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Analyze+

### Contexto

Which of the following were consequences of the Industrial Revolution according to the text? (Select ALL correct options)

## Question 18 (Synthesis of Progress - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-pastlife-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Pragmática + Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
Historian A: "History is written by the victors; we only see one side of the truth."
Historian B: "Archeology and primary sources allow us to rediscover the voices of the common people, regardless of who won the wars."

### Enunciado
What is the most complete synthesis of these two views on historical knowledge?

### Options
- [x] A) While official narratives may be biased toward the powerful, scientific methods and direct evidence can reconstruct a more nuanced and inclusive version of the past. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) History is a complete lie because it is always biased, and we should stop studying it. <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [x] C) Only winners have the right to tell stories, and archeology just confirms their version. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) Both historians are talking about different subjects that have no connection. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the most accurate synthesis (1.0).
- B and C are extreme and reductionist views of the historians' points (0.4 and 0.2).

### Explicación Pedagógica
La opción A reconoce el sesgo (A) pero también la capacidad de rectificación mediante fuentes primarias (B), logrando una síntesis balanceada.

## Question 19 (Historical Meta-cognition - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-pastlife-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Gramatical
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
The concept of "Presentism" in history is the act of judging past actions by modern ethical and social standards. For example, criticizing people from 500 years ago for not having modern views on recycling.

### Enunciado
How can a historian avoid the trap of 'Presentism' when writing a biography of a controversial historical figure?

### Options
- [x] A) By contextualizing the figure's choices within the specific limitations, values, and knowledge of their own time, rather than comparing them to today's norms. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) By assuming that people in the past were just less intelligent than we are now. <!-- weight: 0.3 -->
- [x] C) By ignoring all moral questions and only listing dates and locations of events. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) By changing the dates to make the person seem more modern and likeable. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the correct academic approach to avoid presentism (1.0).
- B and C are flawed methodologies (0.3 and 0.2).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Evitar el presentismo requiere "contextualización" (A), entendiendo que los valores cambian con el tiempo y no se puede juzgar el pasado con la regla del presente sin ser injusto.

## Question 20 (Abstract Continuity - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-pastlife-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Todas
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
A 'Palimpsest' is a piece of parchment that has been erased and used again, but still shows faint traces of the original writing. In urban planning, cities are often seen as palimpsests, where modern skyscrapers stand on the foundations of ancient Roman or medieval walls.

### Enunciado
How can an individual's 'personal biography' be understood as a 'palimpsest'?

### Options
- [x] A) Because current behaviors are built upon the 'erased' but still influential layers of childhood experiences and past versions of oneself. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Because you can simply delete your past and become a completely new person every day. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [x] C) Because life is just a piece of paper that someone else writes on without your permission. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [ ] D) Because parchment is no longer used in the digital age. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A demonstrates extraordinary transfer by applying the architectural/manuscript concept (layering and traces) to psychology and biography (1.0).
- B and C are simplistic interpretations (0.2 and 0.1).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta transfiere un concepto complejo (palimpsesto) a la identidad personal, sugiriendo que el pasado no desaparece del todo, sino que forma la base (faint traces) de lo que somos hoy.
