# Bundle: Daily Routines and Identity

This bundle explores the linguistic and cognitive aspects of routines, habits, and self-organization, ranging from basic identification to complex meta-cognitive evaluation of behavioral patterns.

**ICFES:** Lexical
**Bloom:** Remember

### Enunciado
What is the first thing most people do when they wake up?

### Options
- [x] A) Turn off the alarm
- [ ] B) Cook a formal dinner

## Question 16 (Deeper Premises - Difficulty 6)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-routines-001-PRO-v16`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Analyze+

### Contexto
"The concept of a 'routine' is often misunderstood as a prison of boredom. In reality, it is the framework upon which freedom is built. By automating the mundane decisions—what to wear, what to eat, when to start—we liberate the mind to engage with the complex and the novel. A lack of structure doesn't lead to freedom, but to a perpetual state of decision fatigue."

### Enunciado
Which of the following underlying premises support the author’s argument? (Select ALL correct options)

### Options
- [x] A) Human cognitive resources for decision-making are limited <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Predictability in minor tasks leads to mental energy conservation <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) Spontaneity is the only true form of human intelligence
- [x] D) Structure provides a foundation for higher-level thinking <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] E) People who enjoy routines are generally less creative

### Scoring
- All 3 correct: 2.8/2.8 (100%)
- Correct options: A, B, D.
- Penalty: -0.5 per incorrect (C, E).

### Explicación Pedagógica
El autor asume que "decidir lo mundano" agota la mente (A), que automatizar ahorra energía (B) y que esto libera la mente para lo complejo (D). C y E contradicen la postura del autor.

## Question 17 (Critical Critique - Difficulty 7)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-routines-001-PRO-v17`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Lexical
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
"Atomic Habits" by James Clear suggests that identity-based habits are more effective than outcome-based habits. Instead of saying 'I want to run a marathon' (outcome), one should say 'I am a runner' (identity).

### Enunciado
Which of the following scenarios demonstrate a shift from 'outcome' to 'identity'? (Select ALL correct options)

### Options
- [x] A) A student who refuses a cigarette because they 'are not a smoker', rather than 'trying to quit'. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] B) Someone setting a timer to work for 4 hours to finish a specific book.
- [x] C) An artist who draws every day because it is 'what they do', even when they have no specific project. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] D) A person joining a gym only to lose 5 kilograms for a wedding next month.
- [x] E) A musician playing scales daily because they 'practice their craft', not to prepare for a concert. <!-- weight: 0.7 -->

### Scoring
- Correct: A, C, E.
- Outcome-based: B, D (focus on finishing a book or losing weight for an event).

### Explicación Pedagógica
A, C y E se enfocan en quién es la persona (no fumador, artista, músico), mientras que B y D se enfocan en un resultado externo y finito.

## Question 18 (Cross-Text Synthesis - Difficulty 8)

Text 2: "Technological tools can enhance routines by automating reminders and tracking patterns that were previously invisible to the individual."

Represent the most comprehensive synthesis of how technology impacts human habit formation:

La opción A integra correctamente los aspectos negativos del Texto 1 y los positivos del Texto 2, añadiendo el factor de la "intencionalidad" del usuario.

## Question 19 (Meta-cognitive Paradox - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-routines-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Gramatical
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
The "Hedonic Treadmill" describes the human tendency to quickly return to a relatively stable level of happiness despite major positive or negative events. This implies that even a perfect daily routine might eventually lose its capacity to provide joy due to "habituation" or "boredom."

### Enunciado
How can a person logically resolve the paradox of wanting a 'stable routine' while avoiding the 'happiness decay' of the hedonic treadmill?

### Options
- [x] A) By building a 'meta-routine' that includes scheduled periods of novelty, challenge, and 'deliberate discomfort' to reset the hedonic set point. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) By constantly changing every aspect of their life so the brain never gets used to anything. <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [x] C) By accepting that happiness is impossible and focusing only on productive output within a static routine. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) By ignoring the routine and letting impulses guide every decision. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the most sophisticated and logical resolution (Meta-routine).
- B is a common but chaotic reaction to boredom (0.4).
- C is a stoic but joyless reductionism (0.2).

### Explicación Pedagógica
La paradoja se resuelve no eliminando lo estable, sino incorporando la novedad *dentro* de la estructura (meta-rutina), lo cual permite el crecimiento sin caer en el caos.

## Question 20 (Abstract Transfer - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-routines-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Todas
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
In biology, homeostasis is the state of steady internal physical and chemical conditions maintained by living systems. In software engineering, a 'cron job' is a scheduled task that runs automatically at fixed times.

### Enunciado
How does the concept of a 'human routine' function as both a 'biological homeostasis' and a 'societal cron job'?

### Options
- [x] A) It serves as a regulatory mechanism that reduces internal biological chaos while synchronizing individual behavior with the larger, automated demands of the socio-economic system. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) It is merely a social construct designed by corporations to make people work like machines. <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [x] C) It is a biological instinct that prevents humans from ever doing anything new or dangerous. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) The concepts of biology and software have nothing to do with human psychology. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A demonstrates extraordinary transfer by linking the internal (homeostasis/biology) and external (cron job/software/society) functions of routines (1.0).
- B and C are narrow and reductive (0.4 and 0.2).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta requiere que el estudiante mapee conceptos de dos dominios técnicos (Biología y Computación) a un fenómeno cotidiano (Rutina), reconociendo su función dual de regulación interna y sincronización externa.
