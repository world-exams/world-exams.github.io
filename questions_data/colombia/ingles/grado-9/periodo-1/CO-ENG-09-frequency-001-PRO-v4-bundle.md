# Bundle: The Rhythm of Life (Frequency)

This bundle delves into the nuances of frequency adverbs, temporal consistency, and the psychological structure of habits, moving from simple percentage-based adverbs to complex evaluations of consistency and behavioral momentum.

---

## Question 1 (Basic Adverbs - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Lexical
**Bloom:** Remember

### Contexto
I drink water seven days a week. I never skip a day.

### Enunciado
What adverb best describes this habit?

### Options
- [x] A) Always
- [ ] B) Sometimes
- [ ] C) Rarely
- [ ] D) Occasionally

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Always" indica una frecuencia del 100% (todos los días). Las otras opciones describen frecuencias menores.

## Question 15 (Synthesizing Temporal Patterns - Difficulty 5)

**ICFES:** Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto

Text 2: "Breaking a bad habit requires more energy than forming a new one, because the neural path already exists."

### Enunciado
Combining both texts, why is frequency important in brain development?

### Options
- [x] A) High frequency creates neural automation that becomes difficult to erase later.

## Question 16 (Behavioral Momentum - Difficulty 6)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v16`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Analyze+

### Contexto
"Frequency is the engine of 'Behavioral Momentum'. Just as a heavy train is hard to start but even harder to stop once it's moving, a habit practiced daily builds a psychological force that makes skipping a day feel physically uncomfortable. This inertia is the secret of high performers: they don't use 'willpower' every day; they use the momentum they built through relentless frequency."

### Enunciado
Which of the following describes the concept of 'Behavioral Momentum' as presented? (Select ALL correct options)

### Options
- [x] A) It is built through the repetition of actions over time. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) It reduces the need for active willpower in the long run. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) It makes stopping a habit easier than starting one.
- [x] D) It creates a psychological resistance to breaking the routine. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [ ] E) It depends entirely on how the person feels each morning.

### Scoring
- Correct: A, B, D.
- C is the opposite (it's harder to stop). E is incorrect (momentum is bigger than feelings).

### Explicación Pedagógica
El texto afirma que se construye con repetición (A), ahorra fuerza de voluntad (B) y genera incomodidad (resistencia) al romper la rutina (D).

## Question 17 (Frequency Ethics - Difficulty 7)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v17`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Lexical
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
The 'Frequency Illusion' (also known as the Baader-Meinhof phenomenon) occurs when something you recently learned or noticed suddenly seems to appear everywhere. It’s not that the frequency of the object increased, but that your brain's 'selective attention' is now biased toward noticing it.

### Enunciado
Which scenarios illustrate the 'Frequency Illusion'? (Select ALL correct options)

### Options
- [x] A) You learn a new English word and then hear it three times in different movies on the same day. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] B) A store increases its advertising so you see a product more often.
- [x] C) After buying a red car, you start noticing that 'everyone' seems to be driving red cars. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] D) A scientist counts the real number of birds in a sky to find an average.
- [x] E) You think about a friend you haven't seen in years, and then you see their name in a random article. <!-- weight: 0.7 -->

### Scoring
- Correct: A, C, E.
- B is a real increase in frequency, not an illusion. D is objective measurement.

### Explicación Pedagógica
A, C y E son casos donde la percepción de frecuencia cambia debido a un interés previo o atención selectiva, no porque el mundo haya cambiado.

## Question 18 (Synthesis of Consistency - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Pragmática + Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
View A: "The frequency of a habit determines its strength; therefore, you must never miss a single day to maintain its power."
View B: "Rigid frequency leads to burnout. Flexibility—allowing yourself to miss a day occasionally—ensures the routine is sustainable for years."

### Enunciado
What is the most comprehensive synthesis for a sustainable high-performance routine?

### Options
- [x] A) Relentless frequency should be the goal, but integrated flexibility (the 'never miss twice' rule) prevents small failures from becoming permanent collapses. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) You should only do things when you feel like it to avoid any stress or burnout. <!-- weight: 0.3 -->
- [x] C) Perfection is the only way; a single mistake means the entire routine is destroyed forever. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) Frequency and burnout are not related in any way. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the most balanced and effective synthesis (1.0).
- B and C are unsustainable extremes (0.3 and 0.2).

### Explicación Pedagógica
La opción A integra la "frecuencia implacable" (A) con la "sostenibilidad/flexibilidad" (B), proponiendo una regla práctica (no fallar dos veces) que resuelve el conflicto.

## Question 19 (Temporal Paradox - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Gramatical
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
The "Paradox of Choice" in habits: Having too many good habits to perform daily can lead to 'decision paralysis,' where the frequency of each individual habit drops because the total system is overloaded.

### Enunciado
How can a person logically optimize the 'frequency' of their most important habits without falling into the Paradox of Choice?

### Options
- [x] A) By prioritizing 2-3 'keystone habits' and performing them with 100% frequency, while letting secondary habits happen only as 'bonus' activities. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) By trying to do 50 small habits every day for exactly 1 minute each, regardless of their importance. <!-- weight: 0.4 -->
- [x] C) By deleting all habits and doing something completely different every day to stay fresh. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) By waiting for a lot of free time to do all habits at once. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A is the correct strategic optimization (Keystone habits) (1.0).
- B is an example of 'quantity over quality' that usually fails (0.4).

### Explicación Pedagógica
Resolver la paradoja implica filtrar lo esencial (keystone) para asegurar su frecuencia, en lugar de intentar abarcar todo y fallar en la frecuencia general.

## Question 20 (Abstract Transfer - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-frequency-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Todas
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
In physics, 'Frequency' is the number of occurrences of a repeating event per unit of time (measured in Hertz). In music, frequency determines the 'pitch' of a note. In social science, frequency of interaction determines the 'strength' of a social tie.

### Enunciado
How does the 'frequency' of a human habit act as a 'Harmonic' that shapes the 'Identity' of an individual?

### Options
- [x] A) Just as specific frequencies produce unique musical tones, the specific repetition of actions creates a 'vibration' of character that الآخرين perceive as an 'Identity'—a consistent pattern distinguishable from the surrounding noise. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) Identity is just a random collection of thoughts and has no pattern or frequency. <!-- weight: 0.1 -->
- [x] C) Habits are like loud noises that stop people from having an identity. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) Physics and social science are separate and should never be compared. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A demonstrates extraordinary transfer by linking Physics (Vibration/Hertz), Music (Tone/Harmonic), and Sociology (Social Strength/Identity) (1.0).
- B and C are conceptually shallow.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta le exige al estudiante transferir la idea de "frecuencia" como algo que define la naturaleza de un objeto (sonido/átomo) a la formación de la identidad humana, viéndola como un patrón vibrante y consistente (A).
