## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-community-003-v8`

### Contexto
Used to vs Past Simple.
"He **________** to my house yesterday."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb form for a single action in the past.

### Options
- [x] A) came
- [ ] B) used to come
- [ ] C) comes
- [ ] D) was coming

### Explicación Pedagógica
For a single, finished action (yesterday), we use the **Past Simple** (came), not used to (which is for habits).

"Many indigenous communities in Colombia used to live in isolation. Today, they are working together to preserve their languages and ancestral lands. They use traditional knowledge to protect biodiversity, showing the world how to live in harmony with nature."

### Enunciado
What are indigenous communities doing today according to the text?

### Options

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-community-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"I didn't used to like vegetables."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) Because with 'didn't', the 'd' in 'used' must be removed (didn't use to).
- [ ] B) It should be 'I used not like'.
- [ ] C) Vegetables are healthy, everyone likes them.
- [ ] D) It is correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The auxiliary **did** handles the past tense, so we use the base form **use to**: "I **didn't use** to...".
