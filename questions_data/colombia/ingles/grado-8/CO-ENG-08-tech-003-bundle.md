## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-tech-003-v7`

### Contexto
Defining vs Non-defining.
"My computer, **________** is very old, is slow."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct relative pronoun for this non-essential extra information.

### Options
- [x] A) which
- [ ] B) who
- [ ] C) that
- [ ] D) where

### Explicación Pedagógica
In non-defining relative clauses (with commas), we use **which** for things, never 'that'.

### Contexto
Omission of relative pronoun.
"The video **________** we watched was very funny."

### Enunciado
In this sentence, the relative pronoun is optional. Which one can be omitted?

### Options
- [x] A) that

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-tech-003-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Digital citizenship is the ability to use technology responsibly. A good digital citizen is someone who respects others online and who doesn't share fake news. It is important to know which sources are reliable before sharing content that might be harmful."

### Enunciado
According to the text, what does a good digital citizen avoid?

### Options
- [x] A) Sharing fake news.
- [ ] B) Respecting others.
- [ ] C) Using technology at all.
- [ ] D) Using reliable sources.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text says: "A good digital citizen... **who doesn't share fake news**".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-tech-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"This is the man which works in the Apple store."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) 'Which' is for things; for people (man) we must use 'who'.
- [ ] B) It should be 'the man who work'.
- [ ] C) Apple store should be 'Samsung store'.
- [ ] D) It is correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
We use **who** for people. **Which** is strictly for objects or animals. Correct: "This is the man **who** works...".
