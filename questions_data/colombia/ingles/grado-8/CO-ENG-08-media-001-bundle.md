## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-001-v1`

### Contexto
Identifying media professional roles.

### Enunciado
A person who writes news stories for a newspaper or website is a **________**.

### Options
- [x] A) journalist
- [ ] B) cook
- [ ] C) athlete
- [ ] D) gardener

### Explicación Pedagógica
A **journalist** (periodista) is a professional who collects, writes, or distributes news or other current information.

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-001-v9`

### Contexto
Analyzing a news lead.
"Breaking News: New scientific study proves that regular chocolate consumption improves math skills by 50%!"

### Enunciado
Why should a critical reader be **Suspicious** (desconfiado) of this headline?

### Options
- [x] A) Because the claim seems too good to be true and lacks a specific source.
- [ ] B) Because math is more important than chocolate.
- [ ] C) Because the headline is written in English.
- [ ] D) Because 50% is a very small number for a study.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Extraordinary claims without evidence are a common sign of **misinformation** or bias.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-001-v10`

### Contexto
The concept of "Filter Bubbles".

### Enunciado
What is a **Filter Bubble** (burbuja de filtro) on social media?

### Options
- [x] A) An environment where algorithms only show you information that agrees with your personal beliefs.
- [ ] B) A special case for your phone that protects it from soap and water.
- [ ] C) A transparent sphere that astronomers use to look at distant stars.
- [ ] D) A game where you have to pop as many digital bubbles as possible in one minute.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Filter Bubbles** isolate people from diverse opinions by only showing content they already like or believe.
