## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-history-003-v3`

### Contexto
Past Continuous (Structure).
"Yesterday at 5 PM, the archaeologists **________** (dig) in the site."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct past continuous form.

### Options
- [x] A) were digging
- [ ] B) was digging
- [ ] C) digged
- [ ] D) were dig

### Explicación Pedagógica
For plural subjects (archaeologists), we use **were** + verb-**ing**: **were digging**.

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-history-003-v5`

### Contexto
Past Continuous vs Past Simple (Interruption).
"They **________** (eat) dinner when the earthquake started."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct forms for an interrupted action.

### Options
- [x] A) were eating
- [ ] B) ate
- [ ] C) was eating
- [ ] D) have eaten

### Explicación Pedagógica
The long action (eating) is in **Past Continuous**; the short interruption (started) is in Past Simple.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-history-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"They was playing soccer yesterday at 10 AM."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) 'They' is plural, so it should be 'were playing', not 'was'.
- [ ] B) It should be 'They played'.
- [ ] C) 'At 10 AM' is not possible.
- [ ] D) It is correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Subject-verb agreement: **They were** (plural), **He/She/It was** (singular).
