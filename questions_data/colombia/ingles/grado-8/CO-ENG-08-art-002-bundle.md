**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-art-002-v1`

### Enunciado
Choose the correct term.

### Options
- [x] A) musician
- [ ] B) athlete
- [ ] C) pilot
- [ ] D) chef

### Explicación Pedagógica
A **musician** (músico) is involved in making music.

## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

### Contexto
Used to (Affirmative).
"When I was a child, I **________** listen to nursery rhymes."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct phrase for a past habit that doesn't happen now.

### Options
- [x] A) used to
- [ ] B) use to
- [ ] C) was using to
- [ ] D) am used to

### Explicación Pedagógica
"**Used to**" expresses past habits or states.

Used to (Negative).
"I **________** like jazz before, but now I love it!"

### Enunciado
Choose the correct negative form for a past habit.

### Options
- [x] A) didn't use to
- [ ] B) didn't used to

- [ ] D) used to not

### Explicación Pedagógica
In negatives with "didn't", we use the base form "**use to**" (without -d).

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-art-002-v10`

### Contexto
Correcting a sentence.
"I used to playing the piano every day."

### Enunciado
What is the correct form?

### Options
- [x] A) I used to play the piano every day.
- [ ] B) I used to played the piano every day.
- [ ] C) I am used to play the piano every day.
- [ ] D) I use to play the piano every day.

### Explicación Pedagógica
"Used to" is always followed by the **base verb** (play), not the -ing form.
