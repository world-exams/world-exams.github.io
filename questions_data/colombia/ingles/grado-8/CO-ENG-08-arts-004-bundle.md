## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-arts-004-v1`

### Contexto
Vocabulary: Art forms.
"A painting made on a large wall is called a **________**."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct word.

### Options
- [x] A) mural
- [ ] B) sketch
- [ ] C) statue
- [ ] D) photo

### Explicación Pedagógica
A **mural** is a piece of artwork painted or applied directly on a wall.

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-arts-004-v5`

### Contexto
Present Perfect vs Past Simple.
A: "**________** you **________** (see) the new mural yet?"
B: "Yes, I **________** (see) it on Monday."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct pair of tenses.

### Options
- [x] A) Have you seen / saw
- [ ] B) Did you see / have seen
- [ ] C) Have you seen / seen
- [ ] D) Do you see / saw

### Explicación Pedagógica
Experience (yet) -> **Present Perfect**. Specific time (on Monday) -> **Past Simple**.

Present Perfect (For / Since).
"The museum has been open **________** 10 AM, but I have waited here **________** two hours."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct pair of time prepositions.

### Options
- [x] A) since / for
- [ ] B) for / since

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Digital art has transformed the way artists express themselves. For centuries, painters used only brushes and canvases. Since the 2000s, many have adopted tablets and AI-powered software. This shift has opened new possibilities but has also started a debate: Can a machine truly create art?"

### Enunciado
What shifted in the 2000s for many painters?

### Options
- [x] A) They started using tablets and software.
- [ ] B) They stopped creating art forever.
- [ ] C) They decided to use only old brushes.
- [ ] D) They completely ignored AI.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text reflects: "...many have **adopted tablets and AI-powered software**."

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-arts-004-v10`

### Contexto
Sentence Order.
"never / has / she / been / such / an / to / exhibition / amazing"

### Enunciado
Order the words to form an enthusiastic sentence.

### Options
- [x] A) She has never been to such an amazing exhibition.
- [ ] B) She never has been to such amazing an exhibition.
- [ ] C) Amazing she has never been to such exhibition.
- [ ] D) To such an amazing exhibition never she has been.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Structure: **Subject + has + never + been + to + such an + adjective + noun.**
