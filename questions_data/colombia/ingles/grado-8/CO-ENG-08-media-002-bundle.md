# Base Question: Using Social Media Wisely

> **Source:** Nigeria Curriculum (Adapted for Colombia)
> **Topic:** Social Media Etiquette (Period 2)
> **Level:** A2+ (Elementary/Pre-intermediate)

---

## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v1`

### Contexto
Vocabulary.
"Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok are types of **________**."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct category for these apps.

### Options
- [x] A) social media
- [ ] B) operating systems
- [ ] C) search engines
- [ ] D) hardware

### Explicación Pedagógica
These apps belong to the **social media** (redes sociales) category.

## Question 2 (Muy Fácil B - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v2`

### Contexto
Vocabulary.
"To share a picture on your profile, you **________** it."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb for sharing content online.

### Options
- [x] A) post (or upload)
- [ ] B) delete
- [ ] C) print
- [ ] D) break

### Explicación Pedagógica
To share content online is to **post** (publicar) or upload it.

## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v3`

### Contexto
Obligation (Must).
"You **________** be careful with the information you share online."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct modal for a strong obligation/recommendation.

### Options
- [x] A) must
- [ ] B) mustn't
- [ ] C) needn't
- [ ] D) shouldn't

### Explicación Pedagógica
"**Must**" expresses a strong necessity or duty.

## Question 4 (Fácil B - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v4`

### Contexto
Prohibition (Mustn't).
"You **________** share your password with anyone."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct modal for something that is forbidden or very bad.

### Options
- [x] A) mustn't
- [ ] B) must
- [ ] C) can
- [ ] D) have to

### Explicación Pedagógica
"**Mustn't**" (no debes) is used for prohibition or strong negative advice.

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

### Contexto
Obligation (Have to).
"To create an account, you **________** provide an email address."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct modal phrase for an external requirement.

### Options
- [x] A) have to
- [ ] B) has to
- [ ] C) mustn't
- [ ] D) shouldn't

### Explicación Pedagógica
"**Have to**" expresses a rule or requirement from outside (the app's rules).

No necessity (Don't have to).
"You **________** post a photo every day. It's not a rule."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct phrase for something that is not necessary.

### Options

- [ ] B) mustn't

Civility online.
"If someone is mean to you online, you **________** ignore them and report the user."

### Enunciado

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v8`

### Contexto
Privacy settings.
"If you want to keep your photos private, you **________** (change) your settings to 'Private'."

### Enunciado
Complete the first conditional sentence about a plan.

### Options
- [x] A) will have to (or should)
- [ ] B) changed
- [ ] C) changes
- [ ] D) changing

### Explicación Pedagógica
First conditional: If + Present -> **Will + Modal/Verb** (will have to).

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v9`

### Contexto
Critical analysis.
"Which of these actions is **illegal** on social media?"

### Enunciado
Choose the correct prohibition.

### Options
- [x] A) Creating a profile using someone else's photos without permission.
- [ ] B) Liking too many posts in one hour.
- [ ] C) Following more than 100 friends.
- [ ] D) Posting a video of your cat.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Impersonation or using intellectual property/photos without permission is generally against rules and can be **illegal**.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-08-media-002-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"She doesn't **________** verify her identity if she uses her phone."

### Enunciado
Complete the negative obligation form for "She".

### Options
- [x] A) have to
- [ ] B) has to
- [ ] C) must
- [ ] D) having to

### Explicación Pedagógica
After "doesn't", we use the base form **have to**, even for the third person singular.
