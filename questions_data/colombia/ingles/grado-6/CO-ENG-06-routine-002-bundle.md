# Base Question: Telling Time and Daily Schedule

> **Source:** Nigeria Curriculum (Adapted for Colombia)
> **Topic:** Daily Life (Period 2)
> **Level:** A1 (Beginner)

---

## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v1`

### Contexto
Looking at a clock.
The clock shows: **7:00 AM**.

### Enunciado
What time is it?

### Options
- [x] A) It’s seven o'clock.
- [ ] B) It’s seven past three.
- [ ] C) It’s six o'clock.
- [ ] D) It’s half past seven.

### Explicación Pedagógica
When the minute hand is on 12, we use "**o'clock**" (en punto).

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v2`

### Contexto
Describing a morning action.
"I **________** my teeth every morning."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb.

### Options
- [x] A) brush
- [ ] B) eat
- [ ] C) sleep
- [ ] D) fly

### Explicación Pedagógica
We use "**brush**" (cepillar) for teeth.

## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v3`

### Contexto
Talking about school schedule.
"I go to school **________** 8:00 AM."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct preposition of time.

### Options
- [x] A) at
- [ ] B) on
- [ ] C) in
- [ ] D) to

### Explicación Pedagógica
We use "**at**" for specific times (e.g., at 7:00, at noon).

## Question 4 (Fácil B - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v4`

### Contexto
Describing an evening routine.
"She **________** dinner at 6:30 PM."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb form for 'She'.

### Options
- [x] A) eats (or has)
- [ ] B) eat
- [ ] C) eating
- [ ] D) eaten

### Explicación Pedagógica
For he/she/it in Simple Present, we add **-s** to the verb (eats/has).

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v5`

### Contexto
Asking about time.
"________ ________ is it?"

### Enunciado
Complete the question to ask the time.

### Options
- [x] A) What time
- [ ] B) Where time

Sequencing activities.
"First I wake up, **________** I take a shower."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct word for sequence.

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v7`

### Contexto
Reading a complex time.
The clock shows: **10:45**.

### Enunciado
How do you say 10:45?

### Options
- [x] A) It’s a quarter to eleven.
- [ ] B) It’s a quarter past ten.
- [ ] C) It’s half past ten.
- [ ] D) It’s ten forty.

### Explicación Pedagógica
10:45 is "**a quarter to eleven**" (un cuarto para las once).

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v8`

### Contexto
Using adverbs of frequency.
"I **________** late for school. I am always on time!"

### Enunciado
Choose the correct adverb.

### Options
- [x] A) never
- [ ] B) always
- [ ] C) usually

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v9`

### Contexto
Reading a short paragraph about routine.
"On weekends, Sarah doesn't wake up early. She stays in bed until 10:00 AM, then she has a big breakfast with her family."

### Enunciado
What does Sarah do on weekends?

### Options
- [x] A) She sleeps late and eats with her family.
- [ ] B) She goes to school at 7:00 AM.
- [ ] C) She plays football in the park before breakfast.
- [ ] D) She never eats breakfast.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Understanding a text requires connecting "doesn't wake up early" with "sleeps late".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-routine-002-v10`

### Contexto
Correcting a sentence.
"Does he watches TV in the afternoon?"

### Enunciado
What is the correct form of this question?

### Options
- [x] A) Does he watch TV in the afternoon?
- [ ] B) Do he watch TV in the afternoon?
- [ ] C) Does he watching TV in the afternoon?
- [ ] D) Is he watches TV in the afternoon?

### Explicación Pedagógica
In questions with **Does**, the main verb returns to the base form (watch), without **-s**.
