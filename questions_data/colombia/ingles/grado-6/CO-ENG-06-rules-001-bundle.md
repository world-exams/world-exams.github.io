## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-rules-001-v1`

### Contexto
Expressing polite requests.

### Enunciado
What is the most polite word to use when you ask for something?

### Options
- [x] A) Please
- [ ] B) Now
- [ ] C) Give
- [ ] D) Why

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Please** (por favor) is the essential word for making polite requests.

Asking for permission.

### Enunciado
Which phrase do you use to ask for permission to enter a room or interrupt a conversation?

### Options
- [x] A) Excuse me
- [ ] B) I'm sorry

### Enunciado

### Options

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-rules-001-v9`

### Contexto
Using "must not" vs "don't have to".
"Tomorrow is Sunday. I ________ go to school."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct expression for lack of obligation.

### Options
- [x] A) don't have to
- [ ] B) must not
- [ ] C) should not
- [ ] D) can't

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Don't have to** (no tengo que) indicates it is not necessary. "Must not" would mean it is strictly forbidden.
