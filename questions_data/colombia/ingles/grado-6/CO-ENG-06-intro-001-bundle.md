## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-intro-001-v1`

### Contexto
Asking for basic identity.

### Enunciado
Which question do you ask when you want to know a person's name?

### Options
- [x] A) What is your name?
- [ ] B) How are you?
- [ ] C) Where are you from?
- [ ] D) How old are you?

### Explicación Pedagógica
"What is your name?" (¿Cómo te llamas?) is the standard way to ask for a person's name.

## Question 2 (Muy Fácil B - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-intro-001-v2`

### Contexto
Asking for age.

### Enunciado
If you want to know how many years a person has lived, what do you ask?

### Options
- [x] A) How old are you?
- [ ] B) What is your age? (Less common)
- [ ] C) When are you born?
- [ ] D) How many years do you have? (Direct translation error)

### Explicación Pedagógica
In English, we say "How old are you?" to ask about age.

Asking about a family member's occupation.

### Enunciado
What does your father ________?

### Options
- [x] A) do
- [ ] B) does
- [ ] C) doing
- [ ] D) did

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-06-intro-001-v10`

### Contexto
Logical spelling of names.
"My name is Sarah. That's S - A - R - A - H."

### Enunciado
How many letters are in Sarah's name?

### Options
- [x] A) Five
- [ ] B) Four
- [ ] C) Six
- [ ] D) Three

### Explicación Pedagógica
Counting the letters: S(1), A(2), R(3), A(4), H(5). Total: **Five** (cinco).
