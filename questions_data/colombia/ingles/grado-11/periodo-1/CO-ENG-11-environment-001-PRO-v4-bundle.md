Early attempts to establish (2) _______ emission reduction targets, such as the 1997 Kyoto Protocol, achieved mixed results. The primary flaw of Kyoto was that it only required developed nations to reduce their emissions, while rapidly (3) _______ economies like China and India were exempt.

Recognizing the need for a universally applicable (4) _______, 196 parties adopted the Paris Agreement in 2015. Unlike Kyoto's top-down mandates, Paris uses a bottom-up approach. Each country determines its own "Nationally Determined Contribution" (NDC) and pledges to (5) _______ specific goals. This flexibility brought nearly all nations to the negotiating (6) _______.

The overarching goal of the agreement is to limit global warming to well (7) _______ 2 degrees Celsius compared to pre-industrial levels. However, scientists consistently (8) _______ that current NDCs are insufficient to meet this target. Without drastic and immediate cuts to fossil fuel consumption, vulnerable populations will face severe (9) _______ ranging from sea-level rise to extreme droughts. Consequently, the success of Paris relies heavily on diplomatic peer pressure and the expectation that countries will steadily (10) _______ their ambition over time.

## Master Context B: The Economics of Carbon Pricing (Questions 11-20)

To achieve the emission reductions promised in international agreements, governments must employ economic mechanisms that penalize pollution. Two of the most common (11) _______ are carbon taxes and cap-and-trade systems. A carbon tax simply imposes a direct fee on the carbon content of fossil fuels, thereby raising the (12) _______ of emitting greenhouse gases and encouraging companies to invest in cleaner alternatives.

Cap-and-trade, on the other hand, operates by creating a market for pollution. The government sets a strict (13) _______ on total permissible emissions. It then issues a corresponding number of "allowances" or credits. Companies under the cap must (14) _______ enough credits to cover their emissions. If a factory pollutes less than its allotted amount, it can (15) _______ its extra credits to a heavier polluter.

Proponents argue that this system provides financial (16) _______ for innovation. However, critics claim that carbon markets are fundamentally flawed. They point out that wealthy corporations easily (17) _______ extra credits, effectively allowing them to buy the "right" to keep polluting, often (18) _______ the expense of local communities living near the factories. Additionally, accurately measuring and auditing emissions remains a massive logistical (19) _______. Ultimately, without a high enough price on carbon, neither system will drive the necessary societal transition away from fossil (20) _______.
