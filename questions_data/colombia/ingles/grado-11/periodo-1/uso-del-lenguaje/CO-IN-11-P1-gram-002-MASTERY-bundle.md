# Bundle Mastery: English Vocabulary and Pragmatics

This bundle focuses on lexical range (Part 1) and situational awareness (Part 2), evaluating the student's ability to use the right words in specific settings and understand public signs and warnings.

### Contexto
You are in a high-end restaurant and you accidentally spill some wine on the person at the next table.

### Enunciado
Which is the most appropriate and polite way to handle the situation in a B2/C1 English-speaking context?

### Options
- [ ] A) "Oh, it's just wine. It will dry soon." <!-- feedback: Incorrect. This is dismissive and rude. -->
- [ ] B) "I'm so sorry. I'll get some water for you." <!-- feedback: While better, it's slightly informal for a "high-end" setting. -->
- [x] C) "I'm terribly sorry. Please accept my absolute apologies. I'll call the waiter to help and I'll certainly cover the cleaning costs." <!-- feedback: Correct. Includes a formal apology and an offer to rectify the damage, matching the B2 level requirement for social interaction. -->
- [ ] D) "You shouldn't have been sitting so close to me." <!-- feedback: Incorrect. Shifting blame is unacceptable. -->

### Explicación Pedagógica
Sociolinguistic competence. Evaluating the appropriacy of language registers and cultural expectations in social gaffes.
