This bundle focuses on advanced grammatical structures (Part 4 and Part 6/7 style), idiomatic expressions, and text coherence, aimed at reaching B2 proficiency levels in the Saber 11 exam.

## Part 4: Grammatical Cloze
Read the text and choose the correct word for each space.

### Text: The Future of Urban Mobility
Cities around the world are (1) ____ rapid changes in their transportation systems. Technology is (2) ____ a key role in making commuting more efficient. For instance, electric scooters have become very popular (3) ____ their low environmental impact. However, some experts argue that cities (4) ____ invest more in public transit rather than individual solutions (5) ____ they want to solve traffic congestion permanently.

Geologists are currently debating whether we have entered a new epoch: the Anthropocene. Since the Industrial Revolution, human activity has significantly altered the planet's atmospheric chemistry and biodiversity. ### Contexto
Some people argue that labeling our era as the "Anthropocene" is arrogant because it puts humans at the center of Earth's history.

### Enunciado
Based on the text, what would be the best counter-argument for a scientist who supports the "Anthropocene" label?

### Options
- [x] A) The label is not about arrogance, but about **accountability**: acknowledging our role as a major geological impactor is a step toward fixing the damage. <!-- feedback: Correct. It transforms a label of "dominance" into a label of "responsibility". -->
- [ ] B) Humans are indeed the most important species, so they deserve their own era. <!-- feedback: This confirms the arrogance accusation instead of countering it. -->
- [ ] C) Geology is a very old science and needs new names to stay exciting. <!-- feedback: Superficial argument. -->
- [ ] D) The term was invented by a Nobel Prize winner, so it must be correct. <!-- feedback: Logical fallacy (Appeal to Authority). -->

### Explicación Pedagógica
Critical thinking and stance evaluation. The student must generate an advanced justification for a scientific position within a socio-linguistic debate.
