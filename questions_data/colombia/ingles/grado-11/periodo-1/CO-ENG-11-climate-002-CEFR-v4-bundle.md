## 📖 Grammar Context: Conditionals in Environmental Arguments

The following sentences and cloze passages focus on using conditional structures accurately and appropriately in the context of climate change discussion. Students at B2-C1 must master not just the form of conditionals but their **pragmatic function** — expressing regret, probability, impossibility, and policy recommendations.

## Question 1 — **CEFR: B1+** | Difficulty: 5
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-climate-002-CEFR-v1`
**Skill:** Grammar — Zero Conditional identification

**Enunciado:** Which sentence correctly uses the **Zero Conditional** to describe a scientific fact about climate change?

**Options:**
- [x] A) "If Arctic ice melts, sea levels rise."
- [ ] B) "If Arctic ice melted, sea levels would rise."
- [ ] C) "If Arctic ice had melted, sea levels would have risen."

## Question 11 — **CEFR: B2+** | Difficulty: 8
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-climate-002-CEFR-v11`
**Skill:** Grammar — Conditional wish structures

**Enunciado:** Which sentence correctly uses "I wish" to express regret about a **present situation**?

**Options:**
- [x] A) "I wish governments were more aggressive in their climate commitments."
- [ ] B) "I wish governments would have been more aggressive in their commitments."
- [ ] C) "I wish governments had been more aggressive in their past commitments." *(This expresses past regret, not present)*
- [ ] D) "I wish governments will be more aggressive in the future."

> **Note:** Option C is structurally correct for past regret but option A is the answer for **present** situation regret.
