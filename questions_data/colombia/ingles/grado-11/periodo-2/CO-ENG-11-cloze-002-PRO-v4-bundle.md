## Text 1 (Questions 1-4): The Silent Epidemic
*(Difficulty 3-4)*
In recent years, the dramatic increase in sleep deprivation among teenagers has become a major public health concern. Medical professionals attribute this trend (1) ______ the pervasive use of smartphones late at night. The blue light emitted by these devices suppresses melatonin production, (2) ______ makes falling asleep significantly harder. Furthermore, many students feel pressured to stay up late (3) ______ they can finish their excessive homework loads. If schools (4) ______ their starting times by just one hour, researchers argue, adolescent health would improve dramatically.

### Options

- [ ] D) with

### Explicación Pedagógica
The verb "attribute" is always paired with the preposition "to" in active structures ("attribute something to something else"). It indicates the cause or origin of the trend. Options A, C, and D are incorrect preposition collocations for this specific verb.

### Contexto
*See Text 1, Gap (2)*: ...melatonin production, (2) ______ makes falling asleep significantly harder.

### Enunciado
Select the correct relative pronoun to fill in the blank (2):

### Options
- [ ] A) what
- [x] B) which
- [ ] C) that
- [ ] D) who

### Explicación Pedagógica
The blank introduces a non-defining relative clause (indicated by the preceding comma) that refers to the entire previous fact (the suppression of melatonin production). "Which" is the only correct relative pronoun to refer back to a complete clause in this manner. "That" cannot follow a comma. "What" does not refer back to antecedents. "Who" is only for people.

### Options
- [ ] A) in order to
- [ ] B) due to
- [ ] C) as long as
- [x] D) so that

### Explicación Pedagógica
The blank must connect an action ("stay up late") with a result/purpose clause containing a modal verb ("they *can* finish"). ## Text 2 (Questions 5-9) : The Paradox of Choice
*(Difficulty 4-5)*
Psychologist Barry Schwartz coined the term "paradox of choice" to describe a modern phenomenon. While choice is fundamental to human autonomy, an overwhelming abundance of options (5) ______ actually lead to decision paralysis. When consumers face dozens of varieties of a single product, they often feel exhausted (6) ______ empowered. Schwartz argues that (7) ______ we restrict our choices artificially, we are bound to experience constant dissatisfaction, constantly wondering (8) ______ we made the optimal decision. ### Options

- [ ] B) ought to

### Contexto

### Enunciado
Select the correct word to introduce the embedded question in blank (8):

### Options
- [ ] A) that
- [x] B) whether
- [ ] C) what
- [ ] D) regarding

### Explicación Pedagógica
The verb "wonder" introduces an indirect (embedded) yes/no question. In formal English, "whether" (or "if") is used to introduce such clauses ("I wonder *whether* I did it right"). Option A ("that") is used for statements of fact ("I know that..."), not questions. "What" is for open questions ("wondering what to do"), not a binary choice. "Regarding" is a preposition, not a conjunction for clauses.

### Enunciado
Select ALL the transition words that correctly fit blank (10) both logically and grammatically (mind the preceding comma).

### Options
- [x] A) but <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) yet <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) however
- [ ] D) nevertheless
- [x] E) though <!-- weight: 1.0 -->

### Scoring
- All 3 correct, 0 wrong: 3.0/3.0 (100%)
- 2 correct, 0 wrong: 2.0/3.0 (67%)
- Each wrong selection: -0.25

### Explicación Pedagógica
The blank joins two independent clauses with a contrastive relationship, directly following a comma. "But" and "yet" act as coordinating conjunctions and are grammatically perfect here.
