## Section 3: Advanced Register Transformation

## Question 16 — **CEFR: B2+** | Difficulty: 8
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-register-002-CEFR-v16`

**Enunciado:** Transform the casual complaint to formal written complaint register: "This is completely unacceptable! I paid for next-day delivery and my package took five days to arrive!"

**Options:**
- [ ] A) "I am very upset that my package took so long to arrive despite next-day delivery."
- [x] B) "I am writing to formally express my dissatisfaction with a recent delivery experience. Despite selecting next-day delivery, my order did not arrive until five days after the anticipated date, which represents a failure to fulfill the terms of the service contracted."
- [ ] C) "My package arrived late and this was not acceptable at all because I paid for fast delivery."
- [ ] D) "I was extremely unsatisfied when my purchase took more time than promised in the delivery options."

## Question 20 — **CEFR: C1** | Difficulty: 10
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-register-002-CEFR-v20`

**Enunciado:** A colleague says: "Register isn't just about using longer words — it's about performing a social role." Which of the following sentences BEST illustrates this claim at C1 level?

**Options:**
- [x] A) Text A (formal corporate report) doesn't use formal language merely for precision — it performs the institutional authority of a professional organization; its passive constructions, hedged claims, and quantified evidence collectively assert: "We are credible analysts." Register is a social performance of identity and relationship, not merely a vocabulary inventory.
- [ ] B) Register is only about word choice; longer or more obscure words always indicate a more formal register regardless of context.
- [ ] C) All texts perform a social role, but informal register performs it more naturally because it reflects how people actually speak.
- [ ] D) Social roles are performed through dress codes and behavior; language register is a separate academic concept with no connection to social identity.
