## Question 1 (Standard Application - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v1`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
The university board has made a final decision regarding the new library opening hours.

### Enunciado
Choose the passive transformation that best preserves the meaning and tense of the context:

### Options
- [x] A) A final decision regarding the new library opening hours has been made by the university board.
- [ ] B) A final decision regarding the new library opening hours was made by the university board.
- [ ] C) A final decision regarding the new library opening hours is being made by the university board.
- [ ] D) A final decision regarding the new library opening hours had been made by the university board.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The original sentence uses the present perfect active ("has made"). The correct passive transformation must retain this tense ("has been made"). Option B incorrectly uses the simple past, C uses present continuous, and D uses past perfect.

## Question 2 (Standard Application - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v2`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
The construction team will complete the new stadium by next December.

### Enunciado
Select the most accurate passive sentence:

### Options
- [ ] A) The new stadium will complete by next December.
- [x] B) The new stadium will be completed by next December by the construction team.
- [ ] C) The new stadium would be completed by next December by the construction team.
- [ ] D) The new stadium is being completed by next December.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The context uses the simple future ("will complete"). In passive voice, this becomes "will be completed". Option A lacks the passive verb form entirely, changing the stadium into the agent. Option C unnecessarily shifts to conditional ("would be"), and D uses present continuous.

## Question 3 (Standard Application - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v3`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical
**Bloom:** Apply

### Contexto
"People say that the new policy will drastically reduce inflation."

### Enunciado
Which impersonal passive structure correctly rephrases the statement?

### Options
- [ ] A) The new policy is said to drastically reduce inflation.
- [ ] B) It said that the new policy will drastically reduce inflation.
- [x] C) It is said that the new policy will drastically reduce inflation.
- [ ] D) The new policy was said to will drastically reduce inflation.

### Explicación Pedagógica
"It is said that..." is the standard grammatical form for an impersonal passive construction in the present tense. Option A is structurally possible ("The new policy is said to...") but leaves an awkward verb placement, though C directly mirrors the "It + passive reporting verb + that" pattern which is standard and directly translates "People say that...". Option B lacks the "is" auxiliary, and D mixes past and future awkwardly.

## Question 6 (Advanced Analysis - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v6`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical + Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Analyze

### Contexto
After the scandal broke out, the CEO was forced to resign immediately. It is widely believed that he had embezzled corporate funds.

### Enunciado
Which sentence reformulates the belief into a personal passive construction?

### Options
- [ ] A) It is believed that he has embezzled corporate funds.
- [x] B) He is widely believed to have embezzled corporate funds.
- [ ] C) He was widely believed to embezzle corporate funds.
- [ ] D) He is widely believed to embezzle corporate funds.

### Explicación Pedagógica
When reshaping "It is believed that..." into a personal passive starting with the subject ("He"), we use "He is believed...". Because the embezzlement happened in the past prior to the belief ("had embezzled"), the perfect infinitive "to have embezzled" is strictly required. Option D uses the present infinitive, implying the embezzlement is ongoing.

## Question 7 (Advanced Analysis - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v7`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Analyze

### Contexto
Management did not grant us access to the classified files during the investigation.

### Enunciado
Which passive transformation correctly handles the double object while maintaining the formal tone?

### Options
- [ ] A) The classified files were not granted to us by management during the investigation.
- [x] B) We were not granted access to the classified files during the investigation.
- [ ] C) Access to the classified files was not been granted to us during the investigation.
- [ ] D) Management was not granted access to the classified files by us.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The verb "grant" takes two objects: the indirect object "us" and the direct object "access". In English, it is much more natural and common to make the indirect object the subject in passive transformations ("We were not granted access..."). Option A is grammatically acceptable but sounds clunky and unnatural compared to B. Option C contains a grammatical error ("was not been"), and D completely reverses the meaning.

## Question 8 (Advanced Analysis - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v8`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical
**Bloom:** Analyze

### Contexto
They should have informed the investors about the impending bankruptcy much earlier.

### Enunciado
What is the correct passive form highlighting the investors' missed opportunity?

### Options
- [ ] A) The investors should be informed about the impending bankruptcy much earlier.
- [ ] B) The investors must have been informed about the impending bankruptcy much earlier.
- [x] C) The investors should have been informed about the impending bankruptcy much earlier.
- [ ] D) The investors had been informed about the impending bankruptcy much earlier.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The modal perfect active "should have informed" expresses a past unfulfilled obligation. To change it to the passive, we use "should have been informed". Option A refers to a present/future obligation. Option B ("must have been") expresses a logical deduction about the past, changing the meaning entirely. Option D removes the obligation aspect entirely.

Nobody had ever warned the explorers about the hidden dangers of the cavern.

## Question 13 (Expert Evaluation - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-passive-001-PRO-v13`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical + Lexical
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Having finalized the contract, the manager sent copies to all stakeholders.

### Enunciado
How can the participle clause "Having finalized the contract" be transformed into a passive participle clause?

### Options
- [ ] A) The contract being finalized, copies were sent to all stakeholders.
- [ ] B) Having been finalizing the contract, copies were sent to all stakeholders.
- [x] C) Having been finalized, the contract was copied and sent to all stakeholders.
- [ ] D) Finalized the contract, copies were sent to all stakeholders.

### Explicación Pedagógica
To make a perfect participle active clause ("Having finalized") passive, we use "Having been finalized". Crucially, the subject of the main clause must match the subject inferred from the participle clause to avoid a dangling modifier error. Therefore, "the contract" must be the subject of the main clause, making C the only grammatically coherent and technically accurate choice.
