Based on the context, what does the word **engrossed** mean?

### Options
- [ ] A) Bored and uninterested in technology. <!-- feedback: "Bored" contradicts the context where they are ignoring others to focus on the phone. -->

## Question 2 (Intermedio - Dificultad 5)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P2-tech-001-MASTERY-v2`
**Bloom:** Aplicar
**ICFES:** Estructura Gramatical (Conditionals)
**Expected_Success:** 0.60

### Enunciado
Complete the dialogue appropriately regarding future scenarios of automation:

**Mark:** "I'm worried about robots taking over our accounting jobs in the next five years."
**Sarah:** "Don't panic just yet. If that _______________, we will simply have to learn new skills to manage those machines instead of doing the manual data entry."

### Options
- [ ] A) will happen <!-- feedback: After "if" in a first conditional sentence, we use the present simple, not future "will". -->
- [x] B) happens <!-- feedback: Correct. Typical First Conditional structure describing a real future possibility: If + Present Simple (happens), + Future (will have to). -->
- [ ] C) would happen <!-- feedback: "Would" creates a second conditional (unlikely/hypothetical), but Sarah's response "we will simply have to" requires the first conditional. -->
- [ ] D) had happened <!-- feedback: Third conditional for past impossible situations, completely wrong for a "next five years" prediction. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **First Conditional for Realistic Future Tech Scenarios (B1+ Grammar).**

Do not let the algorithm answer your performance reviews or draft the apology emails to our angry clients."*

### Enunciado
What is the underlying tone and true message of the company's management in this notice?

### Options
- [ ] A) They are demanding that employees stop using AI for coding immediately because it is stealing human jobs. <!-- feedback: The text says they "celebrate" the AI tools for coding, they are not banning it. -->

Words like "absolute monopoly on Nuance" and forbidding AI for "apology emails" highlight that employees must stop using bots for tasks requiring human tact. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación

## Question 4 (Avanzado - Dificultad 6)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P2-tech-001-MASTERY-v4`
**Bloom:** Evaluar
**ICFES:** Construcción de Párrafos (Cohesion & Coherence)
**Expected_Success:** 0.40

### Enunciado
Read the sentences below and choose the correct logical order to form a coherent paragraph about the "Digital Divide".

1. Furthermore, those without reliable broadband access are systematically excluded from remote job opportunities.
2. The term "digital divide" refers to the gap between demographics and regions that have access to modern information tech and those that don't.
3. Therefore, treating internet access as a luxury rather than a basic human right deepens existing socioeconomic inequalities.
4. However, it is not merely about owning a smartphone; it encompasses the quality of connection and digital literacy skills.

### Options
- [ ] A) 2, 1, 4, 3 <!-- feedback: Places "Furthermore" (1) awkwardly before explaining the deeper issue in (4). -->
- [ ] B) 4, 2, 1, 3 <!-- feedback: Starts with "However", which is a contrasting transition word that cannot begin a paragraph. -->
- [x] C) 2, 4, 1, 3 <!-- feedback: Correct logical flow: Definition (2), deeper explanation/contrast (4), additional negative consequence (1), and a concluding synthesis/result (3). -->
- [ ] D) 3, 2, 4, 1 <!-- feedback: Starts with a conclusion ("Therefore") ruining the expository arc. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Paragraph Organization (B2):** Mastery of discourse markers (However, Furthermore, Therefore) and logical progression of ideas.

## Question 5 (Avanzado - Dificultad 7)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P2-tech-001-MASTERY-v5`
**Bloom:** Sistematizar
**ICFES:** Comprensión de Lectura (Main Idea and Synthesis)
**Expected_Success:** 0.35

### Texto Base
*Article Extract on "The Automation Paradox"*
"It is a common historical fear that machines will eradicate human labor entirely. Yet, what economists call the 'Automation Paradox' suggests otherwise. When ATMs (Automated Teller Machines) were introduced globally in the 1970s, bank tellers were terrified of mass unemployment. Surprisingly, the exact opposite occurred. Because ATMs made it cheaper to operate a bank branch, banks opened hundreds of new branches. Consequently, while the number of tellers per branch fell, the massive explosion in total branches meant that the total number of human tellers actually increased significantly nationwide. The nature of their job simply shifted from handing out cash to relationship management and selling loans."

### Enunciado
According to the text, what is the core mechanism behind the "Automation Paradox"?

### Options
- [x] A) Technology reduces operational costs, which can stimulate business expansion, ultimately creating a higher demand for human workers in evolved roles rather than destroying jobs. <!-- feedback: Perfect synthesis. Cheaper branches -> MORE branches -> MORE tellers needed, but doing different tasks (loans/relationships). -->
- [ ] B) Machines are so expensive that companies go bankrupt trying to buy them, forcing them to hire cheap human labor again as bank tellers. <!-- feedback: Factually opposite. ATMs made operating branches *cheaper*. -->
- [ ] C) Customers hate robots so much that banks were instantly forced to dismantle ATMs and bring humans back to hand out cash. <!-- feedback: Not supported by the text. ATMs stayed and expanded. -->
- [ ] D) Human tellers became obsolete and unemployed, prompting the government to create artificial jobs in new branches to hide the crisis. <!-- feedback: Conspiratorial leap. The banks opened branches because it was cheaper and profitable, not to hide a crisis. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Reading Comprehension (B2+):** Extracting the driving economic hypothesis (Paradox mechanism) from a historical case study text.
