**CEFR Level:** B2 (Questions range B1+ → C1)
**Focus:** Inferential reading of professional texts, identifying purpose/tone/strategy in workplace communication, implicit professional language

## 📖 Master Context A: The Interview (Questions 1–9)

**Interviewer:** "Thank you for coming in today, Ms. Valeria Torres. I see from your CV that you spent two years at Tech Solutions. Could you walk me through what you learned there and why you chose to leave?"

**Candidate:** "Certainly. At Tech Solutions, I had the opportunity to manage three concurrent projects simultaneously, which really forced me to develop strong prioritization and delegation skills. In terms of leaving — the company was going through a period of significant restructuring, and I felt that the direction the department was moving no longer aligned with my longer-term professional development goals. I'm very intentional about growing in areas where I can add genuine value, and I felt my skillset was being underutilized."

**Interviewer:** "You mentioned delegation. Can you give me a specific example of a time you delegated effectively — and what the outcome was?"

**Candidate:** "Absolutely. During the Nexus campaign in Q3 of last year, we had a particularly compressed timeline — about three weeks for what would typically have been a six-week deliverable. I identified which tasks required my specific expertise and which could be handled just as effectively by other team members. I assigned the data-gathering phase to two junior analysts, did a brief skills alignment session with them, and then focused my own time on client relationship management and strategic oversight. The project was delivered on time and received positive client feedback."

## 📖 Master Context B: Professional Email Exchange (Questions 7–10)

**Email 1 — From: Daniel Ortega | To: Sarah Mitchell | Subject: RE: Project Phoenix — Status Update**

*"Hi Sarah,*

*Hope you're well. Quick check-in on Phoenix. We're two weeks from the internal deadline and I'm not seeing the deliverable tracker updated since last Thursday. Could you flag where things stand? Specifically: (1) Are we still on schedule? (2) Any blockers I should know about? (3) Do you need anything from me to keep momentum?*

*Thanks in advance,*
*Daniel"*

**Email 2 — Reply from Sarah Mitchell:**

*"Hi Daniel,*

*Thanks for flagging — you're right to check in. We're approximately 80% complete on the design phase. We hit an unexpected blocker last week: the API documentation from IT was incomplete, which held us up for two days. That's since been resolved, but it puts us about three days behind the original schedule. I'm confident we can recover this with some focused effort this week.*

*I'll have the tracker updated by EOD today. And no additional support needed from you at this point — I'll come back to you if that changes.*

*Best,*
*Sarah"*
