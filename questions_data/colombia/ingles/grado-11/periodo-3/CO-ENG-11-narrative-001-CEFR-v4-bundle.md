## 📖 Master Context A: "The Map of Everything" — Original Story Extract (Questions 1–10)

*Elena had kept the map for thirty years. Her grandmother had pressed it into her hands at the airport in Bogotá, saying only: "When you are lost in the north, this will remind you where you come from." Elena had thought it was sentimental nonsense — the kind of thing old women said when they were afraid of being forgotten.*

*In Minnesota, the map lived in a shoebox under her bed. She did not look at it for twenty-two years. She became someone else — someone who ate casseroles and attended parent-teacher conferences and did not roll her R's. Each morning she woke up speaking English before she finished dreaming.*

*Then her daughter asked, on a Tuesday in October with the first snow just starting: "Mamá, where were you born?"*

*Elena opened the box that night. The map had yellowed at the edges and smelled of something she could no longer name. She unfolded it carefully, as though opening a wound she had been careful not to touch. The towns were written in her grandmother's handwriting — a script so familiar and so strange that Elena laughed and then cried, and then sat on the floor in her pajamas for a long time, neither one nor the other.*

## 📖 Master Context B: Narrative Technique — Multiple Texts (Questions 11–20)
