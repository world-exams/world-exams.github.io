## Question 1 (Intermedio - Dificultad 4)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P3-health-001-MASTERY-v1`
**Bloom:** Comprender
**ICFES:** Reconocimiento de Vocabulario en Contexto (Phrasal Verbs)
**Expected_Success:** 0.65

### Enunciado
Read the conversation between a doctor and a patient:

**Doctor:** "Your blood pressure is extremely high. You really need to **cut down on** the amount of salt and fast food you are eating every week, otherwise, you might have a heart attack."
**Patient:** "I know, Doctor. It's just hard because I eat out every day."

What does the phrasal verb **cut down on** mean in this medical context?

### Options
- [ ] A) To start eating something new immediately. <!-- feedback: "Cut down" means reducing, not starting something new. -->
- [ ] B) To chop food into smaller pieces before swallowing. <!-- feedback: Literal and incorrect translation of "cut". -->
- [x] C) To reduce the amount or quantity of something you consume. <!-- feedback: Correct. In health contexts, "cutting down on" sugar, salt, or fat means eating less of it. -->
- [ ] D) To completely eliminate or stop eating something forever. <!-- feedback: "Cut down on" means reduce; "cut out" means eliminate completely. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Meaning from Context Clues (B1 Level Phrasal Verbs):** Inferring specific idioms related to diet and habits.

## Question 2 (Intermedio - Dificultad 5)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P3-health-001-MASTERY-v2`
**Bloom:** Aplicar
**ICFES:** Estructura Gramatical (Reported Speech)
**Expected_Success:** 0.60

### Enunciado
A psychologist is rewriting her session notes. The teenager originally said:
*"I am feeling very anxious about my final exams right now."*

How should the psychologist correctly document this statement using **Reported Speech** in the past tense?

### Options
- [ ] A) The patient said that he is feeling very anxious about his final exams right now. <!-- feedback: Fails to shift the verb tense back (is -> was) and keeps the direct time marker "right now". -->
- [ ] B) The patient said that I was feeling very anxious about my final exams then. <!-- feedback: Incorrect pronoun shift. "I" and "my" should change to "he/she" and "his/her". -->
- [x] C) The patient said that he was feeling very anxious about his final exams at that moment. <!-- feedback: Correct application of backshifting rules: "am feeling" -> "was feeling", "my" -> "his", "right now" -> "at that moment". -->
- [ ] D) The patient said he will feel very anxious about his final exams tomorrow. <!-- feedback: Completely changes the meaning and tense to the future. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Reported Speech Mechanics (B1+ Grammar):** Backshifting tenses, pronouns, and time expressions accurately.

## Question 3 (Avanzado - Dificultad 6)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P3-health-001-MASTERY-v3`
**Bloom:** Analizar
**ICFES:** Lectura Inferencial (Modals of Deduction)
**Expected_Success:** 0.45

### Texto Base
*Observe the situation:*
Emily arrives at school looking incredibly pale. She is shivering, coughing constantly, and holding her throat. She hasn't said a word all morning because she loses her voice every time she tries to speak. 

### Enunciado
Based on the visible evidence, which of the following deductions uses the correct Modal Verb of Certainty?

### Options
- [ ] A) Emily **might** be sick. <!-- feedback: "Might" implies a low probability (maybe yes, maybe no). The evidence (pale, shivering, coughing) shows she is definitely sick. -->
- [ ] B) Emily **can't** be sick. <!-- feedback: This means it is impossible that she is sick, which contradicts all visual evidence. -->
- [x] C) Emily **must** be sick. <!-- feedback: Correct. "Must" + bare infinitive is used for strong logical deductions in the present when we are almost 100% sure based on obvious evidence. -->
- [ ] D) Emily **should** be sick. <!-- feedback: "Should" is used for advice or expectations, not for logical deductions of present facts. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Modals of Deduction/Certainty (B2):** Distinguishing degrees of probability based on contextual evidence (Must vs. Might vs. Can't).

## Question 4 (Avanzado - Dificultad 6)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P3-health-001-MASTERY-v4`
**Bloom:** Evaluar
**ICFES:** Construcción de Párrafos (Cohesion & Contrast)
**Expected_Success:** 0.40

### Enunciado
Read the sentences below and choose the logical order to form a coherent paragraph about "Mindfulness in Schools".

1. Despite this proven success, many traditional academies still refuse to allocate class time for mental health practices.
2. In recent years, several progressive schools have introduced daily 10-minute mindfulness and meditation sessions for their students.
3. They argue that the curriculum is already too crowded with core subjects like Mathematics and Science.
4. As a result of these sessions, teachers have reported a significant drop in aggressive behavior and an increase in student concentration.

### Options
- [ ] A) 2, 1, 4, 3 <!-- feedback: Places the negative restriction (1) before explaining the positive results (4) that the word "success" refers to. -->
- [ ] B) 4, 2, 1, 3 <!-- feedback: Starts with a result ("As a result") without first introducing the action (the meditation sessions). -->
- [x] C) 2, 4, 1, 3 <!-- feedback: Perfect logical flow: Introduction of the practice (2), its positive results (4), the contrast/refusal by others (1), and the reason for their refusal (3). -->
- [ ] D) 1, 3, 2, 4 <!-- feedback: Starts with "Despite this proven success" without having mentioned any success previously. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Paragraph Organization (B2):** Mastery of discourse markers (Despite, As a result, They argue) and contrasting idea progression.

## Question 5 (Avanzado - Dificultad 7)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P3-health-001-MASTERY-v5`
**Bloom:** Sintetizar
**ICFES:** Comprensión de Lectura (Main Idea and Synthesis)
**Expected_Success:** 0.35

### Texto Base
*Excerpt from a Psychology Journal regarding "The Halo Effect"*
"When we meet someone who is physically attractive, our brains often automatically jump to the conclusion that they must also be intelligent, kind, and trustworthy. This cognitive bias is known in psychology as the 'Halo Effect'. It occurs because the human brain struggles to evaluate complex traits independently and prefers cognitive shortcuts. If one outstanding positive feature is immediately visible—such as beauty or height—it casts a 'halo' over our perception, leading us to blindly assume that all their hidden personality traits are equally positive. Consequently, attractive politicians often win more votes, and handsome defendants in court frequently receive lighter prison sentences, simply because juries subconsciously equate beauty with innocence."

### Enunciado
According to the psychological concept of the "Halo Effect" described in the text, what is the fundamental flaw in human judgment?

### Options
- [ ] A) Humans are legally blind and cannot see personality traits at all in courtrooms. <!-- feedback: A literal and absurd misinterpretation of "blindly assume". -->
- [x] B) Humans incorrectly allow a single, obvious positive external trait to dictate their perception of a person's entire character, bypassing critical evaluation. <!-- feedback: Excellent synthesis. The text states that a visible positive feature (beauty) creates a shortcut where we assume all other traits (intelligence, innocence) are also good. -->
- [ ] C) Attractive people are scientifically proven to be more intelligent and kinder than average people. <!-- feedback: The text states this is an assumption (a bias), not a scientific fact. -->
- [ ] D) Politicians win elections solely because they are intelligent, completely ignoring their physical appearance. <!-- feedback: The text states the exact opposite: attractive politicians win more votes because of their looks. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Reading Comprehension (B2+ Synthesis):** Abstracting the core definition of a psychological cognitive bias from a descriptive text with examples.
