## Section 1: Integrated Grammar — Year Review (All Four Periods)

## Question 1 — **CEFR: B2** | Difficulty: 6
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-capstone-002-CEFR-v1`
**Skill:** Present Perfect (P2) + Passive (P4) integrated

### Enunciado
"The rights of indigenous communities _____ systematically violated over decades by extractive industries operating without FPIC compliance." Choose the most appropriate form:

### Opciones
- [x] A) have been
- [ ] B) were
- [ ] C) have
- [ ] D) have had

### Explicación Pedagógica
The present perfect passive "have been violated" is required to describe an action that started in the past and continues to have relevance or effect in the present.

## Question 5 — **CEFR: B2** | Difficulty: 7
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-capstone-002-CEFR-v5`
**Skill:** Cleft sentence (P3 editorial grammar) for argument emphasis

### Enunciado
Rewrite to emphasize INSTITUTIONAL REFORM: "Institutional reform is what climate action most urgently needs."

### Opciones
- [x] A) "What climate action most urgently needs is institutional reform."
- [ ] B) "It is what climate action needs — institutional reform most urgently."
- [ ] C) "Climate action is needing institutional reform most urgently."
- [ ] D) "What that climate action needs is institutional reform most urgently."

### Explicación Pedagógica
A cleft sentence using "What..." refocuses the information to provide more emphasis on the subject (institutional reform).

## Question 7 — **CEFR: B2+** | Difficulty: 8
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-capstone-002-CEFR-v7`
**Skill:** Noun clause (that-clause) for academic distancing

### Enunciado
A student writes: "I believe this argument is weak." Transform to impersonal academic register without removing the claim:

### Opciones
- [x] A) "It can be argued that this position rests on premises that have not been adequately substantiated."
- [ ] B) "This argument is believed by some to be weak, but I agree with this."
- [ ] C) "It seems that there are some who think this argument is weak."
- [ ] D) "Arguably, some might say that perhaps the argument is not the strongest."

### Explicación Pedagógica
Academic register uses impersonal structures like "It can be argued that..." to maintain objectivity.

## Question 17 — **CEFR: B2+** | Difficulty: 9
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-capstone-002-CEFR-v17`
**Skill:** Identifying C1 grammar across year topics

### Enunciado
Which sentence demonstrates the HIGHEST grammatical complexity while remaining perfectly correct?

### Opciones
- [x] A) "Whilst the claim that AI will generate equivalent employment to what it displaces may prove correct in aggregate over time, the distributional question — of who bears the transition cost, where they are located, and whether the timeline of disruption is commensurable with their capacity to adapt — remains both empirically under-examined and politically inconvenient."
- [ ] B) "Although AI may create new jobs, the people who lose jobs because of AI might not be able to get the new ones easily."
- [ ] C) "AI could create jobs, but it could also destroy them, and we don't know which will happen."
- [ ] D) "AI is both good and bad for employment depending on what kind of job you have."

### Explicación Pedagógica
Option A contains multiple subordinate clauses, advanced conjunctions ("whilst"), and sophisticated punctuation to manage complex information.

## Question 20 — **CEFR: C1** | Difficulty: 10
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-capstone-002-CEFR-v20`
**Skill:** Meta-cognitive final question — integration of language learning and critical thinking

### Enunciado
A recent university study found that students who scored high on "linguistic critical thinking" — the ability to analyze language as a vehicle of power — were better equipped for civic engagement, professional decision-making, and ethical reasoning than students with only technical language competency. Does this year's English experience support or challenge that finding?

### Opciones
- [x] A) Support: the year's reading did not merely develop technical language competence (vocabulary, grammar, reading speed) but built a multilayered analytical practice — reading for argument structure, recognizing rhetorical strategy, connecting texts across disciplines, applying ethical frameworks to real-world scenarios. If linguistic critical thinking IS this capacity, then the course was designed precisely to cultivate it, and every bundle built a component of the same integrated practice.
- [ ] B) Challenge — technical language competency is more important than critical thinking for professional success.
- [ ] C) The finding is irrelevant to Grade 11 English — it applies only to university-level study.
- [ ] D) Support, but only because the ICFES exam tests critical thinking, making it directly beneficial for university entrance.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Option A correctly identifies the course's objective of developing critical analytical skills alongside linguistic competence.
