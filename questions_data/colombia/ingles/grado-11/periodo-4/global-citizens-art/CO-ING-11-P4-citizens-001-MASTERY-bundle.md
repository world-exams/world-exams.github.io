## Question 1 (Intermedio - Dificultad 4)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P4-citizens-001-MASTERY-v1`
**Bloom:** Comprender
**ICFES:** Reconocimiento de Vocabulario en Contexto (Phrasal Verbs)
**Expected_Success:** 0.65

### Enunciado
Read the conversation between two exchange students discussing their host country:

**Carlos:** "I really like it here, but sometimes it's hard to communicate. I feel like I'm not learning the language fast enough."
**Anya:** "Don't worry! If you just **keep on** practicing every day and watching local TV shows, you'll become fluent before you know it."

What does the phrasal verb **keep on** mean in this context?

### Options
- [ ] A) To stop doing something because it is too difficult. <!-- feedback: This is the opposite of the advice given ("Don't worry!"). -->
- [ ] B) To store or save something in a secure place. <!-- feedback: This is a literal manipulation of the word "keep" (as in keeping an object). -->
- [x] C) To continue doing an action or activity without stopping. <!-- feedback: Correct. "Keep on" + verb-ing means to continue or persevere with an action (practicing). -->
- [ ] D) To change your current activity for a completely new one. <!-- feedback: "Keep on" implies maintaining the *same* activity, not changing it. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Meaning from Context Clues (B1 Level Phrasal Verbs):** Inferring idioms related to persistence and ongoing actions.

## Question 2 (Intermedio - Dificultad 5)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P4-citizens-001-MASTERY-v2`
**Bloom:** Aplicar
**ICFES:** Estructura Gramatical (Passive Voice)
**Expected_Success:** 0.60

### Enunciado
A museum guide is preparing the description placad for a famous painting that was stolen but later recovered. She wants to emphasize the *action* and the *painting itself*, not the identity of the thieves (which is unknown anyway).
*Active sentence:* "Thieves stole the famous painting from the Louvre in 1911."

How should the guide rewrite this sentence using the **Passive Voice** appropriate for a formal museum plaque?

### Options
- [ ] A) The famous painting is stolen from the Louvre in 1911 by thieves. <!-- feedback: Incorrect tense. "1911" requires the past tense, not the present ("is stolen"). -->
- [x] B) The famous painting was stolen from the Louvre in 1911. <!-- feedback: Correct. Uses the past passive ("was stolen") and omits the unnecessary agent ("by thieves") since the focus is on the object and the event. -->
- [ ] C) The famous painting has been stolen from the Louvre in 1911. <!-- feedback: "Has been stolen" (Present Perfect) cannot be used with a specific finished time in the past like "in 1911". -->
- [ ] D) From the Louvre, thieves were stealing the famous painting in 1911. <!-- feedback: This just rearranges the active voice into the past continuous; it is not the passive voice. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Passive Voice Mechanics (B1+ Grammar):** Correctly transforming active to passive in the Simple Past, recognizing when to omit the agent for formal/academic tone.

## Question 3 (Avanzado - Dificultad 6)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P4-citizens-001-MASTERY-v3`
**Bloom:** Analizar
**ICFES:** Uso del Lenguaje (Relative Clauses)
**Expected_Success:** 0.45

### Enunciado
Read the two sentences about a famous author:
1. Gabriel García Márquez won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982.
2. Gabriel García Márquez's novel *One Hundred Years of Solitude* is a masterpiece of magical realism.

Which of the following Options correctly combines these two sentences using a **Non-defining Relative Clause**?

### Options
- [ ] A) Gabriel García Márquez, who won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982, wrote the novel *One Hundred Years of Solitude* that is a masterpiece. <!-- feedback: Grammatically messy and changes the structure of sentence 2. It doesn't correctly use the possessive relative pronoun. -->
- [ ] B) Gabriel García Márquez, which novel *One Hundred Years of Solitude* is a masterpiece, won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982. <!-- feedback: "Which" cannot be used to show possession for a person. -->
- [x] C) Gabriel García Márquez, whose novel *One Hundred Years of Solitude* is a masterpiece of magical realism, won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982. <!-- feedback: Correct. Uses "whose" to show possession (his novel) and encloses the non-essential extra information in commas. -->
- [ ] D) Gabriel García Márquez that won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982 wrote a masterpiece novel called *One Hundred Years of Solitude*. <!-- feedback: "That" cannot be used in a non-defining relative clause (a clause set off by commas, though commas are missing here too), and it's structurally awkward. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Relative Clauses (B2):** Mastering the use of the possessive relative pronoun "whose" within formal, non-defining clauses common in literary contexts.

## Question 4 (Avanzado - Dificultad 6)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P4-citizens-001-MASTERY-v4`
**Bloom:** Evaluar
**ICFES:** Construcción de Párrafos (Cohesion & Connectors)
**Expected_Success:** 0.40

### Enunciado
Read the sentences below and choose the logical order to form a coherent paragraph about "Street Art".

1. Furthermore, they argue that it brings color and cultural dialogue to otherwise gray and neglected urban areas.
2. For decades, graffiti was strictly classified as vandalism and aggressively removed by city councils.
3. However, the rise of internationally recognized artists like Banksy has initiated a shift in public perception.
4. Today, many art critics view complex murals not as a crime, but as a legitimate and powerful form of contemporary expression.

### Options
- [ ] A) 4, 1, 2, 3 <!-- feedback: Places the historical context (2) after the modern result (4), confusing the timeline. -->
- [ ] B) 3, 4, 1, 2 <!-- feedback: Starts with a transition word ("However") without establishing what it is contrasting against. -->
- [x] C) 2, 3, 4, 1 <!-- feedback: Perfect logical flow: Historical negative view (2), the turning point/contrast (3), the current positive view (4), and additional supporting arguments for the positive view (1). -->
- [ ] D) 2, 1, 4, 3 <!-- feedback: "Furthermore, they argue" (1) implies a previous argument was made, but sentence 2 is about vandalism, so "furthermore" makes no sense there. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Paragraph Organization (B2):** Establishing chronological and logical flow using turning points (However) and additive connectors (Furthermore).

## Question 5 (Avanzado - Dificultad 7)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-P4-citizens-001-MASTERY-v5`
**Bloom:** Sintetizar
**ICFES:** Comprensión de Lectura (Inference and Author's Stance)
**Expected_Success:** 0.35

### Texto Base
*Excerpt from an essay on "The Digital Citizen"*
"The concept of citizenship has traditionally been tethered to geography—defined by the borders of the nation-state in which one is born. However, the unprecedented connectivity of the 21st century has birthed the 'Digital Global Citizen'. Today's youth are just as likely to organize a climate strike with peers in Tokyo and Berlin via a Discord server as they are to attend a local town hall meeting. While optimists hail this borderless empathy as the dawn of a unified global consciousness, critics warn of 'slacktivism'—where changing a profile picture is mistaken for tangible political action. True digital citizenship requires moving beyond passive consumption of global tragedies on a feed, demanding instead that users leverage their vast networks to enact measurable, grassroots change in their physical communities while maintaining a global perspective."

### Enunciado
Based on the text, what is the author's nuanced stance regarding the phenomenon of the "Digital Global Citizen"?

### Options
- [ ] A) The author completely rejects the idea, believing that true citizenship can only happen through traditional geographical borders and local town hall meetings. <!-- feedback: The author acknowledges the reality and potential of the "digital citizen", they do not reject it completely in favor of only traditional geology. -->
- [ ] B) The author unconditionally celebrates digital citizenship because joining Discord servers is scientifically proven to be more effective than local political action. <!-- feedback: Evaluative leap. The text mentions Discord as an *example* of connection, but does not claim it is *more effective* than local action; in fact, the author wants both. -->
- [x] C) The author recognizes its potential for fostering cross-border empathy but cautions that online awareness must be paired with concrete, local actions to be truly meaningful. <!-- feedback: Excellent synthesis. The author sees the good ("borderless empathy") but warns against "slacktivism", concluding that true citizenship needs both the global network and "measurable, grassroots change in their physical communities." -->
- [ ] D) The author believes that 'slacktivism' is the highest form of global consciousness and that changing profile pictures is the only way to solve international crises. <!-- feedback: A complete misreading. The author explicitly criticizes 'slacktivism' as mistaking shallow actions for real political change. -->

### Rúbrica de Justificación
1. **Reading Comprehension (B2+ Synthesis):** Abstracting the author's balanced/critical perspective from a text that presents contrasting viewpoints (optimists vs. critics).
