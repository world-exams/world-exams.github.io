## Section 1: Future Forms for Career and Technology Prediction

## Question 1 — **CEFR: B1+** | Difficulty: 5
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-future-work-002-CEFR-v1`

### Enunciado
Choose the correct future form for a likely prediction: "In ten years, most routine data entry tasks _____ replaced by AI."

### Opciones
- [x] A) will have been
- [ ] B) will be replacing
- [ ] C) are going to replace
- [ ] D) will replace

### Explicación Pedagógica
The future perfect passive "will have been replaced" is used to describe an action that will be completed by a specific time in the future.

## Question 16 — **CEFR: B2+** | Difficulty: 9
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-future-work-002-CEFR-v16`

### Enunciado
Match each term to its most precise definition in the AI/future-of-work context:
1. **Augmentation** 2. **Displacement** 3. **Reskilling** 4. **Upskilling**

### Opciones
- [x] A) 1-Augmentation: AI enhances human capability without replacing the human; 2-Displacement: the human role becomes redundant due to automation; 3-Reskilling: learning an entirely new set of skills for a different occupation; 4-Upskilling: developing deeper or more advanced skills within a current role.
- [ ] B) All four terms describe different stages of the same automation process.
- [ ] C) Augmentation and upskilling are identical; displacement and reskilling are identical.
- [ ] D) These are marketing terms with no precise technical meaning.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Precision in terminology is crucial for understanding socio-economic debates about technology.

## Question 17 — **CEFR: C1** | Difficulty: 9
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-future-work-002-CEFR-v17`

### Enunciado
A student writes: "AI will probably create as many jobs as it destroys." What epistemic challenge should they address to make this a defensible academic claim?

### Opciones
- [x] A) They need to specify: in what timeframe, accessible to which workers, in which geography, at what wage levels, and measured by whose definition of a "job" — without these conditions, the claim is an aggregate prediction that may be true in the long run but misleading for the populations most affected in the short and medium term.
- [ ] B) They should add the source of the McKinsey reports to make the claim credible.
- [ ] C) The claim is already defensible because it uses "probably" as a hedge.
- [ ] D) They should replace "probably" with "certainly" to make the claim stronger.

### Explicación Pedagógica
C1 level requires recognizing the limitations of broad generalizations and specifying variables.

## Question 18 — **CEFR: C1** | Difficulty: 9
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-future-work-002-CEFR-v18`

### Enunciado
How would you rewrite this prediction at C1 level with appropriate epistemic nuance: "AI will take everyone's jobs but maybe not creative ones."

### Opciones
- [x] A) "While evidence suggests that AI-driven automation will restructure labor markets substantially over the coming decades — with roles requiring routine cognitive tasks particularly susceptible to displacement — the extent to which roles demanding creative synthesis, ethical judgment, and affective labor will be affected remains an empirically contested question dependent on the pace and nature of further AI development."
- [ ] B) "There is no doubt that AI will displace all workers except those in creative industries."
- [ ] C) "AI will probably take many jobs, although creative jobs might be protected in some cases."
- [ ] D) "Research shows AI could displace workers in many sectors, including some creative roles."

### Explicación Pedagógica
Option A uses sophisticated syntax and academic hedges to present a balanced view.
