### Opciones
- [ ] A) News
- [ ] B) Gossip
- [x] C) Disinformation

### Contexto
**Article headline:** "Scientists say chocolate cures all diseases!"
### Enunciado
**What should make you skeptical?**
### Opciones
- [ ] A) Scientists are mentioned

### Opciones

- [ ] B) Someone's opinion article

## Question 10 (Critical Thinking)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-literacy-103-v10`


**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-literacy-103-v10`

### Contexto
**Claim:** "I saw it on the news, so it must be true."
### Enunciado
**What is the flaw in this reasoning?**
### Opciones
- [ ] A) News is always 100% accurate.
- [x] B) It assumes all news sources are equally reliable and never make mistakes.
- [ ] C) Television never lies.
- [ ] D) The news is always fake.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Critical thinking means questioning sources, even reputable ones. No source is infallible.
*Español: El pensamiento crítico significa cuestionar fuentes, incluso las respetables. Ninguna fuente es infalible.*
