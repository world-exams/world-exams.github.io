### Contexto
**Short Text:**
"The trolley problem is a thought experiment in ethics. A runaway trolley will kill five people unless you pull a lever to divert it onto a different track, where it will kill one person. Utilitarians argue you should pull the lever (saving five is better than saving one). Deontologists argue you shouldn't (actively causing death is wrong, even if it saves more lives)."

### Enunciado
**According to the text, why might someone refuse to pull the lever?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Because saving five lives is wrong.
- [x] B) Because actively causing a death is considered morally wrong.
- [ ] C) Because the person doesn't understand the problem.
- [ ] D) Because utilitarianism forbids action.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** Deontologists argue "actively causing death is wrong"—the action itself is prohibited regardless of outcome.

The philosophical concept **"categorical imperative"** (Kant) means:

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Rules that only apply sometimes
- [x] B) A moral principle that applies universally, regardless of personal desires

## Question 9 (Challenge - Ethical Reasoning)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-philosophy-010-v9`

ID: CO-ENG-10-philosophy-010-v9

### Contexto
**Scenario:**
A doctor has five patients dying for lack of organs. A healthy visitor comes in for a checkup. Should the doctor kill the one to save the five?

### Enunciado
**Which ethical principle would OPPOSE this action?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Pure utilitarianism (maximize lives saved)
- [x] B) The principle that people should not be used merely as means to others' ends
- [ ] C) The belief that doctors should help no one
- [ ] D) The idea that five deaths are acceptable

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** Kant's principle: treat people as ends, not merely as means. Using someone as an "organ source" violates their dignity.
