## Question 5 (Part 5 - Correlation vs Causation - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-media-bias-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-media-bias-001-v5

### Contexto
**Claim:**
"Ice cream sales increase in summer. Shark attacks increase in summer. Therefore, ice cream causes shark attacks."

### Enunciado
What logical error is this?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It is a fact.
- [ ] B) Circular reasoning.
- [x] C) Confusing correlation with causation.
- [ ] D) Ad hominem.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Both increase due to a third factor (Heat), not because one causes the other. **Correlation is not causation**.
*Español: Correlación no implica causalidad.*

## Question 7 (Part 7 - Framing - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-media-bias-001-v7`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-media-bias-001-v7

### Contexto
**Two headlines about the same event:**
1. "Youth protestors demand climate action."
2. "Truant teens disrupt traffic in city center."

### Enunciado
How do the headlines differ?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) They describe different events.
- [x] B) They use different 'framing' to influence opinion (Positive vs Negative).
- [ ] C) They are identical.
- [ ] D) Neither is in English.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Framing**: Same event, different angle. Headline 1 frames them as activists (positive); Headline 2 frames them as criminals/nuisance (negative).
*Español: Framing (Encuadre) para influir opinión.*
