## Question 2 (Part 2 - Methodology Flaw - Difficulty 5)

ID: UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v2

### Contexto
**Study:** "We asked 5 people in our office if they like coffee. 100% said yes. Conclusion: Everyone in the world likes coffee."

### Enunciado
What is the problem with this study?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The questions were too hard.
- [x] B) The sample size is too small and biased.

## Question 4 (Part 4 - Variable Identification - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v4`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v4

### Contexto
**Experiment:** "We water Plant A with water and Plant B with soda to see which grows taller."

### Enunciado
What is the **Independent Variable** (the thing we change)?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The height of the plants.
- [x] B) The type of liquid (water vs soda).
- [ ] C) The pot size.
- [ ] D) The sunlight.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Independent Variable = The cause/input we change (**Liquid**). Dependent Variable = The result (Height).
*Español: Identificación de variables experimentales (Independiente).*

## Question 5 (Part 5 - Correlation Trap - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v5

### Contexto
**Data:** "Tall children usually have bigger vocabularies."

### Enunciado
Does being tall CAUSE you to know more words?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Yes, height improves memory.
- [ ] B) No, vocabulary makes you grow.
- [x] C) No, both increase with age (a third variable).
- [ ] D) Yes, short people cannot read.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Confounding Variable (Age)**. Older kids are taller AND know more words. Height doesn't cause vocab.
*Español: Variable de confusión (Edad).*

## Question 6 (Part 6 - Reading Abstracts - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v6`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v6

### Contexto
**Abstract:** "Results indicate a significant but weak correlation (r=0.2) between x and y."

### Enunciado
Does X strongly determine Y?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Yes, it is significant.
- [ ] B) Yes, r=0.2 is huge.
- [x] C) No, the correlation exists but it is weak.
- [ ] D) X has no relationship to Y.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Understanding stats terms. "Significant" means *reliable*, not *strong*. "Weak correlation" means X doesn't strongly determine Y.
*Español: Interpretación estadística (Significativo no es igual a Fuerte).*

## Question 9 (Part 9 - Unit Conversion Logic - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v9`

ID: UNI-ENG-10-scientific-literacy-001-v9

### Contexto
**Fact:** 1 inch = 2.54 cm.

### Enunciado
Is 10 inches longer than 20 cm?

### Opciones
- [x] A) Yes (10 in = 25.4 cm).
- [ ] B) No (10 in = 10 cm).
- [ ] C) No (20 cm is clearly bigger than 10).
- [ ] D) They are equal.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Math: 10 * 2.54 = 25.4 cm. 25.4 > 20. So **Yes**.
*Español: Conversión de unidades y comparación.*
