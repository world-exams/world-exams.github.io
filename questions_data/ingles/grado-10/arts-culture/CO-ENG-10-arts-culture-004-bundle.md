**Choose the correct words for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [x] A) (1) which, (2) has been

## Question 6 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-arts-culture-004-v6`

ID: CO-ENG-10-arts-culture-004-v6

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"When Banksy's shredded artwork sold for $25 million—more than the original price before it self-destructed—the art world revealed its paradox: rebellion is profitable. The artist mocks the system; the system monetizes the mockery. Nothing escapes the market, not even critique of the market itself."

### Enunciado
**What paradox does the author identify?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Art should be free for everyone.
- [x] B) Anti-capitalist art becomes more valuable within capitalism.
- [ ] C) Banksy is not a real artist.
- [ ] D) Markets always fail.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** The "paradox" is that Banksy's critique of commercialism made the work MORE valuable commercially—capitalism absorbs its critics.

### Enunciado

ID: CO-ENG-10-arts-culture-004-v10

### Contexto
**Statement:**
"Art should comfort the disturbed and disturb the comfortable."

### Enunciado
**What does this statement suggest about art's purpose?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Art should only be pleasant and decorative.
- [x] B) Art should provide solace to some while challenging others.
- [ ] C) Art has no purpose.
- [ ] D) Only comfortable people should view art.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** Art has dual functions: offering comfort to those in pain, while challenging complacency in those who are comfortable.
