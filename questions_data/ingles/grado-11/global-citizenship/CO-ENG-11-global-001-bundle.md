## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-global-001-v1`

ID: CO-ENG-11-global-001-v1

### Contexto
**Definitions:**
1. The right to vote in political elections.
2. A person who flees for safety, especially to a foreign country.
3. The interconnectedness of world economies and cultures.
4. Prejudice or discrimination against a person or people.

**Words:**
A. Globalization
B. Refugee
C. Suffrage
D. Bias
E. Tariff

### Enunciado
Match definition **"1. The right to vote in political elections"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [ ] A
- [ ] B
- [x] C
- [ ] D

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C (Suffrage):** From Latin *suffragium*. It is the formal term for the right to vote.
**Why others are wrong:**
- *Globalization* (A) refers to interconnectedness (Definition 3).
- *Refugee* (B) refers to fleeing for safety (Definition 2).
- *Bias* (D) refers to prejudice (Definition 4).

## Question 4 (Part 4 - Grammatical Cloze I)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-global-001-v4`

ID: CO-ENG-11-global-001-v4

### Contexto
**Text:**
"Being a global citizen means understanding that our lives are connected to people (1)______ we have never met. It involves recognizing that our choices impact the planet."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct word for (1).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) which
- [ ] B) whose
- [x] C) whom
- [ ] D) who's

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C (whom):** In formal English, *whom* is used as the object of a verb or preposition. Here, "people [that] we have never met" -> "we have never met [them/whom]".
**Note:** In modern casual English, *who* is often used, but *whom* is the grammatically precise choice tested in B2/C1 exams.

## Question 6 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading - TOEFL Style)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-global-001-v6`

ID: CO-ENG-11-global-001-v6

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"While often criticized for diluting local cultures, globalization has undeniably accelerated the exchange of medical knowledge. In 2020, the rapid sequencing of the viral genome was shared instantaneously across borders, a feat impossible fifty years ago. However, this efficiency creates a paradox: while solutions are global, access remains stubbornly local, tethered to the economic reality of each nation."

### Enunciado
**What does the author imply about the distribution of medical solutions?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It is faster than the distribution of viral genomes.
- [x] B) It significantly lags behind the global sharing of scientific knowledge.
- [ ] C) It has been solved by the efficiency of modern technology.
- [ ] D) It is no longer influenced by a nation's economy.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** The author notes a "paradox": solutions (knowledge) are global/fast, but access (distribution) "remains stubbornly local". This implies a gap or lag between knowledge sharing and actual access.
**TOEFL Inference:** You must connect the "efficiency" of sharing with the "stubborn" reality of access.

## Question 7 (Part 7 - Advanced Cloze)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-global-001-v7`

ID: CO-ENG-11-global-001-v7

### Contexto
**Text:**
"Despite the benefits of cross-cultural exchange, many argue that it leads to homogenization. (1)________, proponents suggest that exposure to diverse cultures actually strengthens one's own identity by providing a point of contrast."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct connector for (1).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Therefore
- [ ] B) In addition
- [x] C) Conversely
- [ ] D) Consequently

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C (Conversely):** The sentence introduces an opposing view ("proponents suggest...") to the previous sentence ("many argue..."). *Conversely* indicates this contrast.
**Distractors:**
- A & D: Indicate cause/effect.
- B: Indicates adding more of the same argument.

"I have never visited such a beautiful country."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct transformation.**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Never before I have visited such a beautiful country.

- [ ] C) Never before I visited such a beautiful country.
