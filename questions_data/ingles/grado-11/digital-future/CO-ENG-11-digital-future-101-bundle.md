## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v1`

ID: CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v1

### Enunciado
**The practice of storing data on remote servers accessed via the internet is called:**

### Opciones
- [x] A) Cloud computing
- [ ] B) Chatbot
- [ ] C) Firewall
- [ ] D) Malware

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Cloud computing" refers to storing and accessing data on remote servers via the internet, rather than on a local hard drive. The "cloud" is a metaphor for the internet.

*Español: "Cloud computing" (computación en la nube) se refiere a almacenar y acceder datos en servidores remotos a través de internet, en lugar de en un disco duro local. La "nube" es una metáfora para internet.*

| English | Español |
|---------|---------|
| Cloud computing | Computación en la nube |
| Chatbot | Bot conversacional |
| Firewall | Cortafuegos |
| Malware | Software malicioso |

## Question 4 (Part 4 - Grammar)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v4`

ID: CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v4

### Contexto
"If technology companies (1)______ more transparent about data collection, users (2)______ make more informed decisions."

### Enunciado
**Complete with the correct verb forms.**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) are, (2) will
- [x] B) (1) were, (2) would
- [ ] C) (1) was, (2) will
- [ ] D) (1) have been, (2) would have

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** This is a **Second Conditional** (hypothetical present/future). Structure: If + past simple, would + base verb.

*Español: Esta es una **Segunda Condicional** (presente/futuro hipotético). Estructura: If + pasado simple, would + verbo base.*

| Type | Structure | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 1st | If + present, will | Real possibility |
| 2nd | If + past, would | Hypothetical |
| 3rd | If + past perfect, would have | Unreal past |

## Question 7 (Part 7 - Advanced Grammar)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v7`

ID: CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v7

### Contexto
"Despite (1)______ significant concerns about AI safety, major tech companies continue to accelerate development. (2)______, smaller startups argue that regulation would stifle innovation."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct words for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [x] A) (1) raising, (2) Meanwhile
- [ ] B) (1) risen, (2) However
- [ ] C) (1) rise, (2) Therefore
- [ ] D) (1) raised, (2) Nevertheless

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Despite" requires a gerund or noun: "despite raising." "Meanwhile" introduces a parallel action by a different group.

*Español: "Despite" (a pesar de) requiere gerundio: "despite raising." "Meanwhile" (mientras tanto) introduce acción paralela de otro grupo.*

| Linker | Use | Ejemplo |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Meanwhile | Simultaneous | She studied; meanwhile, he worked |
| However | Contrast | It's expensive. However, it's worth it |
| Therefore | Result | He prepared. Therefore, he passed |

## Question 9 (Challenge - Sentence Transformation)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v9`

ID: CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v9

### Contexto
**Original:** "People widely believe that AI will transform education."
**Rewrite using:** *IT IS WIDELY...*

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct transformation.**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It is widely believing that AI will transform education.
- [x] B) It is widely believed that AI will transform education.
- [ ] C) It is widely believe that AI will transform education.
- [ ] D) It is widely been believed that AI will transform education.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** The structure "It is + adverb + past participle + that..." is formal passive. "Widely believed" replaces "people widely believe."

*Español: La estructura "It is + adverbio + participio pasado + that..." es pasiva formal. "Widely believed" reemplaza "people widely believe."*

**Pattern / Patrón:**
- People say that... → It is said that...
- Scientists believe that... → It is believed that...

## Question 10 (Challenge - Critical Thinking)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v10`

ID: CO-ENG-11-digital-future-101-v10

### Contexto
**Quote (Shoshana Zuboff):** "Surveillance capitalism unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data."

### Enunciado
**What argument does Zuboff make about the digital economy?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Users willingly sell their data for fair compensation.
- [ ] B) Tech companies respect user consent and boundaries.
- [x] C) Human behavior is extracted and commodified without meaningful consent.
- [ ] D) Surveillance benefits society equally.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Unilaterally claims" = takes without permission. "Free raw material" = not compensated. Zuboff argues our data is taken, not given.

*Español: "Unilaterally claims" = toma sin permiso. "Free raw material" = sin compensación. Zuboff argumenta que nuestros datos son tomados, no dados.*

**Context:** Shoshana Zuboff is a Harvard professor who coined "surveillance capitalism" in 2019.
*Contexto: Shoshana Zuboff es profesora de Harvard que acuñó "capitalismo de vigilancia" en 2019.*
