### Contexto
**Definitions:**
1. False information spread intentionally to deceive.
2. The practice of verifying claims before publishing.
3. A filter that shows content matching existing beliefs.
4. Unbalanced reporting favoring one perspective.

**Words:**
A. Bias
B. Disinformation
C. Echo Chamber
D. Fact-Checking
E. Clickbait

### Enunciado
Match definition **"3. A filter that shows content matching existing beliefs"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [ ] A
- [ ] B
- [x] C
- [ ] D

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C (Echo Chamber):** Social media algorithms create "echo chambers" where users mainly see content that confirms their existing views.

## Question 6 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-literacy-007-v6`

ID: CO-ENG-11-media-literacy-007-v6

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"News organizations face a paradox in the digital age: quality journalism requires time and resources, yet readers expect free content and instant updates. Those who pay reporters to investigate for months struggle against bloggers who can publish unverified claims in minutes. The economic model that once sustained investigative journalism—print advertising—has collapsed, and no clear replacement has emerged."

### Enunciado
**What does the author imply about the future of investigative journalism?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It will be completely replaced by bloggers.
- [ ] B) Print advertising will return to solve the problem.
- [x] C) Its survival depends on finding new funding models.
- [ ] D) Readers are willing to pay any price for quality.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** "No clear replacement has emerged" implies the need for new models. The author presents a problem without an obvious solution—survival requires innovation.

### Enunciado
The term **"confirmation bias"** refers to:

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The tendency to forget information quickly
- [x] B) The tendency to seek information that supports existing beliefs
- [ ] C) The ability to change one's mind easily
- [ ] D) The preference for visual over text content

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** *Confirmation bias* = cognitive tendency to favor information that confirms what you already believe while ignoring contradictory evidence.

## Question 9 (Challenge - Source Analysis)

ID: CO-ENG-11-media-literacy-007-v9

### Contexto
**Two sources:**
- **Source A:** A peer-reviewed scientific journal
- **Source B:** An anonymous blog post

### Enunciado
**Why is Source A generally more reliable?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Blogs are always completely wrong.
- [ ] B) Journals are free to read.
- [x] C) Peer review involves expert verification before publication.
- [ ] D) Journals never make mistakes.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** Peer review = experts in the field evaluate the research before publication, providing quality control. This doesn't mean journals are perfect, but they have verification processes blogs lack.

## Question 10 (Challenge - Critical Thinking)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-literacy-007-v10`

ID: CO-ENG-11-media-literacy-007-v10

### Contexto
**Statement:**
"Freedom of speech does not mean freedom from consequences. You can say what you want, but others can also choose to criticize, boycott, or distance themselves from you."

### Enunciado
**What distinction is the author making?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The government should punish offensive speech.
- [x] B) Legal freedom to speak does not prevent social responses.
- [ ] C) People should never face criticism for their views.
- [ ] D) Free speech is not important.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** The author distinguishes between legal freedom (government can't arrest you) and social consequences (others can still react). Free speech ≠ freedom from criticism.
