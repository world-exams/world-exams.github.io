## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-artificial-intelligence-002-v1`

ID: CO-ENG-11-artificial-intelligence-002-v1

### Contexto
**Definitions:**
1. A set of rules followed by a computer to solve problems.
2. The ability of a machine to imitate intelligent human behavior.
3. A type of AI that learns from data without explicit programming.
4. Ethical concerns about the impact of technology on society.

**Words:**
A. Algorithm
B. Machine Learning
C. Artificial Intelligence
D. Techno-ethics
E. Automation

### Enunciado
Match definition **"3. A type of AI that learns from data without explicit programming"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [ ] A
- [x] B
- [ ] C
- [ ] D

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B (Machine Learning):** ML is a subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data rather than following explicit rules.
**Why others are wrong:**
- *Algorithm* (A) is just a set of instructions (Definition 1).
- *Artificial Intelligence* (C) is the broader field (Definition 2).
- *Techno-ethics* (D) refers to ethical concerns (Definition 4).

## Question 7 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-artificial-intelligence-002-v7`

ID: CO-ENG-11-artificial-intelligence-002-v7

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"The AI revolution differs fundamentally from previous industrial revolutions. While the steam engine and electricity augmented human physical capabilities, AI augments—and potentially replaces—cognitive labor. A factory worker displaced by a robot could retrain for a desk job. But when AI can write reports, analyze data, and even create art, the question becomes: what uniquely human skills remain?"

### Enunciado
**What does the author imply about the future workforce?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) All jobs will be replaced by AI immediately.
- [ ] B) Physical labor will become more valuable than cognitive work.
- [x] C) Workers will need to develop skills that AI cannot replicate.
- [ ] D) Retraining programs are no longer useful.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** The rhetorical question "what uniquely human skills remain?" implies workers must identify and develop such skills. The author doesn't say all jobs disappear (A) or retraining is useless (D).

ID: CO-ENG-11-artificial-intelligence-002-v8

### Contexto
**Text:**
"The ethical implications of AI are (1)______. On one hand, it can diagnose diseases and reduce accidents. (2)______, it raises concerns about surveillance, bias, and the concentration of power in tech corporations."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct words for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) simple, (2) However
- [ ] B) (1) complex, (2) Therefore
- [x] C) (1) complex, (2) On the other hand
- [ ] D) (1) obvious, (2) In addition

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** "On one hand... On the other hand" is a classic contrast structure. The ethical implications are *complex* (not simple or obvious) because they have both positive and negative aspects.
