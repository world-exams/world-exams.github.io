## Question 2 (Grammar)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-human-rights-108-v2`

### Enunciado
**Human rights (1)_______ by many international organizations since the Universal Declaration (2)_______ in 1948.**
### Opciones
- [x] A) (1) have been protected, (2) was adopted
- [ ] B) (1) are protected, (2) adopted
- [ ] C) (1) protected, (2) were adopted

### Explicación Pedagógica

*Español: Pasado perfecto pasivo para un esfuerzo continuo hasta ahora, y pasado simple pasivo para una fecha terminada específica (1948).*

## Question 5 (Pragmatics)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-human-rights-108-v5`

### Contexto
**Protester's Sign:** "Justice delayed is justice denied."
### Enunciado
**What is the message of this sign?**
### Opciones
- [ ] A) Justice is impossible.
- [x] B) For justice to be real, it must happen in a reasonable amount of time.
- [ ] C) We should wait longer for decisions.
- [ ] D) Denying things is good.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** The phrase implies that if a legal remedy is available but not forthcoming in a timely fashion, it is effectively the same as having no remedy at all.
*Español: La frase implica que si un recurso legal está disponible pero no se presenta de manera oportuna, es efectivamente lo mismo que no tener ningún recurso.*

## Question 6 (Inferential)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-human-rights-108-v6`

### Contexto
**Passage:** "A free press acts as a 'watchdog'. While it may sometimes cause discomfort to politicians, its presence is essential for a healthy democracy to expose corruption and inform the public."
### Enunciado
**Why is the press called a 'watchdog'?**
### Opciones
- [ ] A) Because journalists like animals.
- [x] B) Because it observes and reports on the actions of the government to prevent abuse.
- [ ] C) Because it only reports on safe topics.
- [ ] D) Because it is controlled by the police.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** A watchdog protects by sounding an alarm. The press "alarms" the public about corruption or bad governance.
*Español: Un perro guardián protege dando una alarma. La prensa "alarma" al público sobre la corrupción o el mal gobierno.*
