**Definitions:**
1. The protection of fundamental freedoms by law.
2. An official pardon for political crimes.
3. Cruel or abusive treatment of prisoners.
4. The forced removal of people from their homes.

**Words:**
A. Amnesty
B. Torture
C. Displacement
D. Civil Liberties
E. Genocide

### Enunciado
Match definition **"1. The protection of fundamental freedoms by law"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [ ] A
- [ ] B
- [ ] C

**Correct Answer D (Civil Liberties):** Rights protected by law, such as freedom of speech, religion, and assembly. "Civil" = relating to citizens.

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"International human rights frameworks face a fundamental tension: they assert universal values while respecting national sovereignty. When a government violates the rights of its own citizens, external intervention risks accusations of neocolonialism. Yet non-intervention enables atrocities. The 'Responsibility to Protect' doctrine attempts to navigate this paradox, but its selective application—intervention in Libya but not Syria—undermines its credibility."

### Enunciado
**What does the author suggest about international human rights enforcement?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It works perfectly without any problems.
- [ ] B) National sovereignty should always take priority.
- [x] C) Inconsistent application damages the credibility of enforcement.
- [ ] D) Intervention is never justified.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** The author notes "selective application... undermines its credibility"—the inconsistency is the problem, not intervention itself.

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct connectors for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) Therefore, (2) Because

## Question 10 (Challenge - Critical Thinking)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-human-rights-006-v10`

ID: CO-ENG-11-human-rights-006-v10

### Contexto
**Statement:**
"Economic, social, and cultural rights (like the right to healthcare and education) are just as important as civil and political rights (like free speech and voting)."

### Enunciado
**What tension does this statement address?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The difference between rich and poor countries.
- [x] B) Historical prioritization of civil rights over social rights.
- [ ] C) The conflict between religion and science.
- [ ] D) The need to abolish all governments.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** Western democracies historically emphasized civil/political rights, while socialist countries prioritized economic/social rights. The statement argues for equal importance.
