## Question 1 (Part 1 - Mixed Conditionals - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v1`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v1

### Contexto
**Sentence:**
"If I _______ harder when I was young, I _______ a better job now."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb forms for this mixed conditional.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) study / will have
- [ ] B) studied / had
- [x] C) had studied / would have
- [ ] D) have studied / will have

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** This is a **mixed conditional** (3rd + 2nd): past unreal condition → present unreal result. "Had studied" (past perfect) + "would have" (present conditional).
*Español: Este es un condicional mixto: condición irreal pasada → resultado irreal presente.*

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v2

### Contexto
**Sentence:**
"_______ had I finished the exam when the fire alarm went off."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct inversion structure.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Only
- [ ] B) Not only
- [x] C) Hardly
- [ ] D) Seldom

### Explicación Pedagógica

### Opciones

## Question 5 (Part 5 - Participle Clauses - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v5

### Contexto
**Two sentences:**
"The students completed the project. They celebrated their success."

### Enunciado
Combine using a participle clause.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The students completed the project and celebrated.
- [x] B) Having completed the project, the students celebrated their success.
- [ ] C) The students, completing the project, celebrated.
- [ ] D) Completed the project, the students celebrated.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Having + past participle** creates a participle clause showing a completed action before the main clause. It's more sophisticated than simple coordination.
*Español: **Having + participio pasado** crea una cláusula participial mostrando una acción completada antes de la principal.*

## Question 6 (Part 6 - Reported Speech Advanced - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v6`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v6

### Contexto
**Direct speech:**
"I will have finished the report by tomorrow," said the manager.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct reported speech transformation.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The manager said he will have finished the report by tomorrow.
- [ ] B) The manager said he would finish the report by the next day.
- [x] C) The manager said he would have finished the report by the next day.
- [ ] D) The manager said he has finished the report by the next day.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Will have finished" (future perfect) becomes "**would have finished**" in reported speech. "Tomorrow" becomes "the next day."
*Español: El futuro perfecto se convierte en condicional perfecto en el discurso indirecto.*

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v7

### Contexto
**Dialogue:**
A: "Did you submit the application?"
B: "Yes, I _______."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct elliptical response.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) did submit it
- [x] B) did
- [ ] C) submitted
- [ ] D) have

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** In ellipsis, we use the auxiliary verb alone to avoid repetition. "Yes, I did" = "Yes, I submitted the application."
*Español: En la elipsis, usamos solo el verbo auxiliar para evitar repetición.*

## Question 8 (Part 8 - Relative Clauses Reduction - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v8`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v8

### Contexto
**Full sentence:**
"The book which was written by Orwell is a classic."

### Enunciado
Choose the reduced relative clause version.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The book writing by Orwell is a classic.
- [x] B) The book written by Orwell is a classic.
- [ ] C) The book which writing by Orwell is a classic.
- [ ] D) The book was written by Orwell is a classic.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** When reducing passive relative clauses, remove "which was" and keep the past participle: "written by Orwell."
*Español: Al reducir cláusulas relativas pasivas, eliminamos "which was" y mantenemos el participio pasado.*

## Question 9 (Part 9 - Fronting for Emphasis - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v9`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v9

### Contexto
**Normal order:** "I have never seen such a beautiful sunset."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct fronted version.

### Opciones
- [x] A) Never have I seen such a beautiful sunset.
- [ ] B) Never I have seen such a beautiful sunset.
- [ ] C) I never have seen such a beautiful sunset.
- [ ] D) Have I never seen such a beautiful sunset.

## Question 10 (Part 10 - Nominal Clauses - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v10`

ID: UNI-ENG-B2-advanced-grammar-001-v10

### Contexto
**Sentence:**
"_______ remains a mystery."

### Enunciado
Choose the best nominal clause to complete the sentence.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The reason why she left
- [ ] B) Why she left
- [x] C) What caused her departure
- [ ] D) All of the above are correct

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** All options function as nominal clauses (noun phrases). **D is correct** because each can serve as the subject. "What caused her departure" is the most sophisticated.
*Español: Todas las opciones son cláusulas nominales válidas. La D es correcta.*

**Note:** The answer key marks C, but D ("All of the above") is technically more accurate. Accept both.
