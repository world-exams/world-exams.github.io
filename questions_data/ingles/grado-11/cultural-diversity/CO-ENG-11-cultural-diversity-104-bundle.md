## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-cultural-diversity-104-v1`

ID: CO-ENG-11-cultural-diversity-104-v1

### Enunciado
**The coexistence of different cultural groups in a society is called:**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Assimilation
- [x] B) Multiculturalism
- [ ] C) Nationalism
- [ ] D) Segregation

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** *Multiculturalism* celebrates different cultures coexisting. *Assimilation* is when minorities adopt the dominant culture. *Segregation* is forced separation.

*Español: El *multiculturalismo* celebra la coexistencia de diferentes culturas. La *asimilación* es cuando las minorías adoptan la cultura dominante. La *segregación* es separación forzada.*

| Term | Definition | Término |
|------|------------|---------|
| Multiculturalism | Coexistence of cultures | Multiculturalismo |
| Assimilation | Adopting dominant culture | Asimilación |
| Integration | Mutual adaptation | Integración |
| Segregation | Forced separation | Segregación |

### Contexto
**Sign at international event:** "Please be mindful of cultural differences. What is considered polite in one culture may be different in another."

### Enunciado
**What is the sign encouraging?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Everyone should behave the same way
- [x] B) People should be aware and respectful of different cultural norms
- [ ] C) Only the host culture's rules apply
- [ ] D) Cultural differences should be ignored

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Be mindful" = be aware/considerate. The sign promotes cultural sensitivity—understanding that norms vary.

*Español: "Be mindful" = ser consciente/considerado. El letrero promueve sensibilidad cultural—entender que las normas varían.*
