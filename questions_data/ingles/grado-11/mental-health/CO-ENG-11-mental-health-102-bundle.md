## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)

ID: CO-ENG-11-mental-health-102-v1

### Enunciado
**The ability to recover from difficulties is called:**

### Opciones
- [x] A) Resilience
- [ ] B) Burnout
- [ ] C) Anxiety
- [ ] D) Depression

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Resilience" comes from Latin *resilire* (to rebound). It describes the capacity to recover quickly from setbacks—like a rubber ball bouncing back.

*Español: "Resilience" (resiliencia) viene del latín resilire (rebotar). Describe la capacidad de recuperarse rápidamente de los contratiempos.*

| English | Español |
|---------|---------|
| Resilience | Resiliencia |
| Burnout | Agotamiento |
| Anxiety | Ansiedad |
| Well-being | Bienestar |

## Question 3 (Part 3 - Conversation)

ID: CO-ENG-11-mental-health-102-v3

### Contexto
**Friend:** "I've been feeling really overwhelmed with school and family stuff."
**You:** "____________"

### Enunciado
**Choose the most supportive response.**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) That's life, just deal with it.
- [ ] B) You think YOU have problems?
- [x] C) That sounds tough. I'm here if you want to talk.
- [ ] D) Just think positive!

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Option C validates feelings ("sounds tough"), offers support without judgment. Dismissive responses (A, D) are harmful.

*Español: La opción C valida sentimientos ("sounds tough"), ofrece apoyo sin juzgar. Respuestas desestimativas (A, D) son dañinas.*

| Say This ✓ | Avoid This ✗ |
|------------|--------------|
| "I hear you" | "Just get over it" |
| "That sounds hard" | "It could be worse" |
| "I'm here for you" | "You're overreacting" |
