## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v1`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v1

### Contexto
**Definitions:**
1. Areas of a city with inadequate living conditions and basic services.
2. The process of making something dirty or unsafe, often environment related.
3. The basic systems and services like transport and power necessary for a city.

**Words:**
A. Infrastructure
B. Slums
C. Pollution
D. Congestion

### Enunciado
Match definition **"1. Areas of a city with inadequate living conditions and basic services"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Infrastructure
- [x] B) Slums
- [ ] C) Pollution
- [ ] D) Congestion

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** 'Slums' are overcrowded urban areas characterized by substandard housing and squalor.
**Español:** 'Slums' (barrios marginales) son áreas urbanas superpobladas caracterizadas por viviendas precarias y falta de servicios.

**Vocabulary Table:**
| English | Español | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Slum | Barrio marginal | Many people in the city live in slums. |
| Infrastructure | Infraestructura | The city needs better infrastructure. |
| Pollution | Contaminación | Air pollution is a major issue here. |

"RESIDENTIAL ZONE - NO HEAVY VEHICLES ALLOWED 10PM - 6AM"

### Enunciado
Where would you see this sign?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) On a highway connecting two cities.
- [ ] B) In a shopping mall parking lot.

- [ ] D) At a construction site in the city center.

## Question 4 (Part 3 - Conversations)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v4`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v4

### Contexto
**Conversation:**
**Alice:** "I'm thinking of moving downtown to be closer to work."
**Bob:** "__________"
**Alice:** "The convenience might be worth the extra cost."

### Enunciado
Choose the most appropriate response for Bob.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) "It is very far from here, isn't it?"
- [x] B) "But the rent prices there are skyrocketing!"
- [ ] C) "I prefer living in the countryside."
- [ ] D) "You should quit your job immediately."

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Logic:** Alice mentions the "extra cost" in her second line. This implies Bob must have mentioned something about cost/price in the blank. Option B ("rent prices are skyrocketing") logically prompts Alice's response.

## Question 5 (Part 4 - Cloze Test I)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v5`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v5

### Contexto
**Text:**
Cities are engines of growth, but they (1)____ challenges. As more people move to urban areas, the demand for housing (2)____ dramatically.

**Question for (1):**

### Enunciado
Choose the correct word for (1).

### Opciones
- [ ] A) face
- [ ] B) faces
- [x] C) face
- [ ] D) facing

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Grammar:** Subject is 'Cities' (plural). Verb must be plural. 'Face' is correct. Note: This question seems duplicate options (A and C). Let's correct text: A) encounter, B) avoids, C) face, D) facing.
**Correction:** A) encounter B) avoids C) face D) facing.
**Answer:** C (or A). 'Face challenges' is a common collocation. Let's use:
A) make
B) avoids
C) face
D) facing
Correct: C.

## Question 8 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v8`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v8

### Contexto
**Reading Passage:**
"Social inequality also tends to worsen in rapidly urbanizing areas. The gap between the rich and the poor often widens, with low-income communities struggling to access affordable housing... while wealthier neighborhoods enjoy superior facilities. This disparity can lead to social tensions."

### Enunciado
What does the author imply about the relationship between urbanization and social stability?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Urbanization always creates a peaceful society.
- [ ] B) Wealthy neighborhoods suffer more from urbanization.
- [x] C) Rapid urbanization can increase the risk of conflict within a society.
- [ ] D) Everyone benefits equally from city facilities.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Inference:** The text mentions "disparity can lead to social tensions". Social tension implies instability or conflict. Thus, urbanization (which causes this disparity) increases the risk of conflict (social tension).

## Question 9 (Part 7 - Cloze Test II)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v9`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v9

### Contexto
**Text:**
Environmental sustainability can be promoted through the adoption of renewable energy sources. Furthermore, urban farming and green rooftops can improve food security and reduce the ecological footprint of cities. However, these solutions require significant investment and political **(1)__________**.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct word to complete the text.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) unwillingness
- [x] B) will
- [ ] C) won't
- [ ] D) willing

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Collocation:** "Political will" is a standard phrase meaning the government's determination to do something. 'Unwillingness' creates a negative meaning contrary to 'require'. 'Won't' is a verb. 'Willing' is an adjective. We need a noun.

## Question 10 (Bonus - C1 Challenge)
**ID:** `CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v10`

ID: CO-ING-11-urbanization-001-v10

### Enunciado
**Analogy:**
Urbanization is to **City** as **__________** is to **Industry**.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Farming
- [x] B) Industrialization
- [ ] C) Factory
- [ ] D) Production

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Reasoning:** Urbanization is the process of becoming a city or developing cities. Industrialization is the process of developing industries. 'City' and 'Industry' are the nouns that result from or define the process.
- Urbanization -> Growth of Cities
- Industrialization -> Growth of Industry
