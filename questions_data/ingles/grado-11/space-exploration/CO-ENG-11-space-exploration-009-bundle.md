## Question 1 (Part 1 - Vocabulary)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-space-exploration-009-v1`

ID: CO-ENG-11-space-exploration-009-v1

### Contexto
**Definitions:**
1. A celestial body that orbits a star.
2. The curved path an object takes around another object in space.
3. A vehicle designed for travel in outer space.
4. The region beyond Earth's atmosphere.

**Words:**
A. Spacecraft
B. Orbit
C. Exoplanet
D. Outer Space
E. Satellite

### Enunciado
Match definition **"3. A vehicle designed for travel in outer space"** with the correct word.

### Opciones
- [x] A
- [ ] B
- [ ] C
- [ ] D

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer A (Spacecraft):** Any vehicle designed for space travel—rockets, shuttles, capsules. Not to be confused with *satellite* (C), which orbits but isn't designed for human travel.

**Text:**
"The search for extraterrestrial life has focused on finding water, (1)______ it is essential for life as we know it. (2)______, scientists increasingly acknowledge that alien life might function in ways completely unfamiliar to us."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct connectors for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) although, (2) Therefore
- [ ] B) (1) unless, (2) So
- [x] C) (1) since, (2) However

## Question 8 (Challenge - C1 Vocab)

ID: CO-ENG-11-space-exploration-009-v8

### Enunciado
The term **"habitable zone"** (or "Goldilocks zone") refers to:

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The area inside a spacecraft where astronauts live
- [x] B) The distance from a star where liquid water could exist
- [ ] C) A region of space without asteroids
- [ ] D) The training facility for astronauts

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** The *habitable zone* is the orbital distance where temperatures allow liquid water—not too hot (close to star), not too cold (far away). "Goldilocks" = just right.
