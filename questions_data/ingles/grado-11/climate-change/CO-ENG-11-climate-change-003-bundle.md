The phrase **"carbon footprint"** refers to:

## Question 7 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-climate-change-003-v7`

ID: CO-ENG-11-climate-change-003-v7

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"Developing nations face an impossible dilemma. They need economic growth to lift their populations out of poverty, yet traditional development pathways—coal, oil, heavy industry—would accelerate climate catastrophe. Wealthy nations, having already industrialized using fossil fuels, now ask developing countries to take a 'greener' but more expensive path. Without significant financial support and technology transfer, this demand rings hollow."

### Enunciado
**What does the phrase "this demand rings hollow" imply?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The demand is being made loudly and clearly.
- [x] B) The demand is hypocritical without accompanying support.
- [ ] C) Developing nations refuse to listen.
- [ ] D) Climate change is not a real problem.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** "Rings hollow" is an idiom meaning "sounds insincere" or "lacks substance". The author suggests wealthy nations' demands are hypocritical without offering help.

## Question 8 (Part 7 - Advanced Cloze)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-climate-change-003-v8`

ID: CO-ENG-11-climate-change-003-v8

### Contexto
**Text:**
"Renewable energy has become increasingly cost-competitive. (1)______, solar and wind power are now cheaper than coal in many regions. (2)______, the transition faces obstacles: grid infrastructure, energy storage, and political resistance from fossil fuel interests."

### Enunciado
**Choose the correct connectors for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) However, (2) Therefore
- [x] B) (1) In fact, (2) Nevertheless
- [ ] C) (1) Therefore, (2) Because
- [ ] D) (1) Although, (2) So

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** "In fact" introduces supporting evidence (solar/wind are cheaper). "Nevertheless" introduces a contrast (obstacles exist despite cost advantages).
