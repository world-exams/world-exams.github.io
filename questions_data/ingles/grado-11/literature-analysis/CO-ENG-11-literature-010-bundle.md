### Enunciado
**Choose the correct words for (1) and (2).**

### Opciones
- [x] A) (1) which, (2) that

## Question 7 (Part 6 - Inferential Reading)

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"When Orwell wrote '1984' in 1948, its dystopian surveillance state seemed an extreme warning. Today, with facial recognition cameras on every corner and algorithms predicting our behavior, the novel reads less like fiction and more like prophecy. Perhaps literature's greatest power is not prediction but the creation of conceptual language—'Big Brother,' 'Thought Police,' 'Newspeak'—that helps us recognize and resist the realities Orwell feared."

### Opciones

- [ ] D) Describing only historical events

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** The author values literature for "creation of conceptual language... that helps us recognize and resist." It's about giving us vocabulary for understanding, not prediction per se.

### Enunciado

### Opciones
- [ ] A) (1) Because of, (2) Therefore
- [x] B) (1) Beyond, (2) Although

**Correct Answer B:** "Beyond" indicates the novel is MORE than entertainment. "Although" acknowledges the tedious parts while introducing a contrasting scholarly view.

## Question 9 (Challenge - C1 Literary Term)
**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-literature-010-v9`

ID: CO-ENG-11-literature-010-v9

### Enunciado
**"Dramatic irony"** occurs when:

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The narrator lies to the reader
- [ ] B) A character makes a joke
- [x] C) The audience knows something the characters do not
- [ ] D) The ending is happy despite a sad beginning

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer C:** *Dramatic irony* = the audience has knowledge the characters lack. Example: In "Romeo and Juliet," we know Juliet is alive, but Romeo does not.

## Question 10 (Challenge - Critical Thinking)

ID: CO-ENG-11-literature-010-v10

### Contexto
**Statement:**
"To kill a masterpiece, assign it in school. Nothing murders a young person's love of reading faster than mandatory analysis and standardized tests."

### Enunciado
**What critique of education does this statement make?**

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Schools should assign more difficult books.
- [x] B) The way literature is taught can destroy rather than cultivate love of reading.
- [ ] C) Standardized tests are the best way to assess comprehension.
- [ ] D) Young people should never read classic literature.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Correct Answer B:** The statement argues that forced, test-driven reading kills intrinsic motivation. The critique is of *method*, not of reading itself.
