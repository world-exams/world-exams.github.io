## Reference Text:

**Colombia: A Country of Biodiversity**

Colombia is the second most biodiverse country in the world, after Brazil. With coastlines on both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, three branches of the Andes Mountains, and the Amazon rainforest, Colombia has an incredible variety of ecosystems.

The country is home to approximately 10% of the world's species, including 1,900 species of birds—more than any other country. The national bird, the Andean Condor, can be seen flying over the high mountains.

However, this biodiversity faces serious threats. Deforestation, illegal mining, and climate change are affecting many species. Conservation groups are working with local communities to protect these natural treasures.
