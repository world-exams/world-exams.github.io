## Question 1 (Part 1 - Academic Vocabulary - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v1`

ID: UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v1

### Enunciado
**Definition:** To express an idea in a different form while keeping the original meaning.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Plagiarize
- [x] B) Paraphrase
- [ ] C) Summarize
- [ ] D) Annotate

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Paraphrase** means restating something in your own words. Essential for academic writing to avoid plagiarism.
*Español: **Parafrasear** es reformular una idea con tus propias palabras.*

**Vocabulary Table:**
| English | Español | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Paraphrase | Parafrasear | You should paraphrase, not copy directly. |
| Plagiarize | Plagiar | Plagiarizing is academic dishonesty. |
| Summarize | Resumir | Summarize the main arguments. |

## Question 4 (Part 3 - Hedging Language - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v4`

ID: UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v4

### Contexto
**Academic sentence:**
"The results _______ that social media has a negative impact on adolescent mental health."

### Enunciado
Choose the hedging expression that maintains academic caution.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) prove
- [ ] B) show
- [x] C) suggest
- [ ] D) confirm

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Suggest** is hedging language—it avoids absolute claims. Academic writing requires caution; "prove" is too strong without exhaustive evidence.
*Español: **Suggest** es lenguaje cauteloso que evita afirmaciones absolutas.*

**IELTS/CAE Tip:** Use hedging (may, could, suggest, appear to) to sound more academic.

Which thesis statement best addresses the prompt with a clear argument?

### Enunciado
Choose the appropriate reporting verb for introducing a researcher's claim.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Goleman thinks

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 9 (Part 8 - Error Correction - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v9`

ID: UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v9

### Contexto
**Sentence with error:**
"The data shows that most participants was satisfied with the service."

### Enunciado
Identify the grammatical error.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) "shows" should be "show"
- [x] B) "was" should be "were"
- [ ] C) "participants" should be "participant"
- [ ] D) "satisfied" should be "satisfying"

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Participants" is plural, so it requires **were**. "Data" can be treated as singular or plural (U.S. vs. U.K.), but "participants" is clearly plural.
*Español: "Participants" es plural, así que requiere **were**.*

## Question 10 (Part 9 - Synthesis - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v10`

ID: UNI-ENG-C1-academic-writing-001-v10

### Contexto
**Two sources:**
- Source A: "Globalization has led to economic growth in developing nations."
- Source B: "The benefits of globalization are unevenly distributed, often favoring multinational corporations."

### Enunciado
Which sentence best synthesizes both sources?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Globalization is entirely positive for developing countries.
- [ ] B) Globalization has no clear effects on global economies.
- [x] C) While globalization has contributed to economic growth in developing nations, its benefits tend to disproportionately advantage multinational corporations.
- [ ] D) Source A and Source B completely contradict each other.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Synthesis combines ideas from multiple sources into a coherent argument. Option C integrates both perspectives using a concessive structure ("While... its benefits...").
*Español: La síntesis combina ideas de múltiples fuentes en un argumento coherente.*
