## Question 1 (Part 1 - Sophisticated Vocabulary - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v1`

ID: UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v1

### Enunciado
**Definition:** To spread or promote an idea or theory widely.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Procrastinate
- [x] B) Propagate
- [ ] C) Prognosticate
- [ ] D) Prevaricate

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Propagate** means to spread (a report, doctrine, practice, etc.) widely. "Procrastinate" is to delay; "Prognosticate" is to predict; "Prevaricate" is to speak evasively.
*Español: **Propagate** es difundir ampliamente una idea. Los otros son distractores de sonido similar.*

### Opciones

## Question 5 (Part 4 - Inversion & Emphasis - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v5

### Contexto
**Sentence:**
"Little _______ how dangerous the situation was."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct structure to complete the specific inversion.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) they knew
- [x] B) did they know
- [ ] C) have they known
- [ ] D) they did know

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** After negative or restrictive adverbs like "**Little**" at the start of a sentence, we must invert the subject and auxiliary verb: "Little **did they know**."
*Español: Inversión obligatoria después de adverbio negativo inicial.*

## Question 6 (Part 5 - Critical Inference - Difficulty 5)

ID: UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v6

### Contexto
**Passage:**
"The serendipity of the discovery belies the years of rigorous preparation that allowed the scientist to recognize the anomaly not as an error, but as a revelation. Chance favors the prepared mind."

### Enunciado
What is the author's primary argument about scientific discovery?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) All scientific discoveries are purely accidental.
- [ ] B) Preparation is useless without luck.
- [x] C) Luck plays a role, but expertise is required to exploit it.
- [ ] D) Scientists should ignore anomalies to avoid errors.

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 7 (Part 6 - Logical Fallacies - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v7`

ID: UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v7

### Contexto
**Argument:**
"If we ban this specific pesticide, farmers will go bankrupt, food prices will skyrocket, and the economy will collapse."

### Enunciado
Identify the logical fallacy in this argument.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Ad Hominem
- [x] B) Slippery Slope
- [ ] C) Straw Man
- [ ] D) Circular Reasoning

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** A **Slippery Slope** fallacy asserts that a relatively small first step will inevitably lead to a chain of related (negative) events.
*Español: Falacia de "pendiente resbaladiza": asumir consecuencias catastróficas inevitables.*

## Question 10 (Part 9 - Cloze / Proficiency - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v10`

ID: UNI-ENG-C2-global-issues-001-v10

### Contexto
**Text:**
"Ideally, the project should be completed by June. _______, we must prepare for potential delays."

### Enunciado
Choose the connector that introduces a contrasting reality or concession.

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Consequently
- [x] B) Be that as it may
- [ ] C) In the event that
- [ ] D) Henceforth

### Explicación Pedagógica

*Español: Expresión formal equivalente a "sea como fuere" o "no obstante".*
