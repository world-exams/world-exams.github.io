## Question 5 (Part 5 - Validity vs Reliability - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-academic-research-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-academic-research-001-v5

### Contexto
**Scenario:** "The thermometer always reads 50 degrees, even in boiling water."

### Enunciado
How would you describe this measurement?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Valid but not Reliable.
- [x] B) Reliable (Consistent) but not Valid (Accurate).
- [ ] C) Both Valid and Reliable.
- [ ] D) Perfect.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Reliable** = Consistent (Always 50). **Valid** = Accurate (True temp). It gives the same wrong answers -> Reliable but Invalid.
*Español: Fiabilidad (Reliability) vs Validez (Validity).*
