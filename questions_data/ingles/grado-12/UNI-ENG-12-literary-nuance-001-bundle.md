## Question 1 (Part 1 - Verbal Irony - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v1`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v1

### Contexto
**Situation:** Walking into a completely ruined, chaotic room.
**Character says:** "Well, this is organized."

### Enunciado
What does the character mean?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The room is actually clean.
- [ ] B) He is hallucinating.
- [x] C) He is being sarcastic; the room is a mess.
- [ ] D) He likes the chaos.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Sarcasm/Verbal Irony**. Saying the opposite of reality to emphasize the mess.
*Español: Ironía verbal (sarcasmo).*

## Question 2 (Part 2 - Unreliable Narrator - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v2`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v2

### Contexto
**Excerpt:**
"I am the most honest person I know. Everyone else is a liar. I never lie. Believe me."

### Enunciado
How should the reader view this narrator?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Usually trustworthy.
- [ ] B) An objective observer.
- [x] C) With suspicion (Unreliable Narrator).
- [ ] D) As a saint.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Unreliable Narrator**. Over-insistence on their own honesty ("I never lie") is a classic literary flag for a character who is deceiving themselves or the reader.
*Español: Narrador no confiable (exceso de justificación).*

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v3

### Contexto
**Text:**
"The *majestic* ancient oak tree stood *sentinel*, watching over the *fleeting* lives of the humans below."

### Enunciado
What is the tone of this passage?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Comedic and lighthearted.
- [ ] B) Angry and resentful.

**English:** Words like "majestic", "sentinel", "fleeting" create a **solemn/reverent** tone about time and nature.

## Question 5 (Part 5 - Subtext - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v5`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v5

### Contexto
**Dialogue:**
"Are you going to eat *that*?" (Stressed on 'that')

### Enunciado
What is the speaker implying?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Curiosity about the food's name.
- [x] B) Disgust or judgment about the food choice.
- [ ] C) Desire to share the food.
- [ ] D) Hunger.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Subtext**. Emphasis on "that" often implies "that disgusting thing". It changes a question into a judgment.
*Español: Subtexto entonativo (Juicio/Asco).*

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v7

### Contexto
**Situation:** Someone survives a horrific car crash with only a scratch.
**Comment:** "Well, that wasn't ideal."

### Enunciado
What figure of speech is this?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Hyperbole (Exaggeration)
- [x] B) Litotes / Understatement
- [ ] C) Personification
- [ ] D) Simile

**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v8`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v8

### Contexto
**Phrase:**
"A deafening silence filled the room."

### Enunciado
How can silence be deafening?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It was extremely loud.
- [x] B) The silence was so heavy/intense it felt oppressive (Oxymoron).
- [ ] C) Everyone was deaf.
- [ ] D) It is impossible nonsense.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Oxymoron**. Combining contradictory terms ("Deafening" vs "Silence") to describe a powerful emotional atmosphere.

## Question 9 (Part 9 - Dramatic Irony - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v9`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v9

### Contexto
**Scene:** The audience knows the killer is hiding in the closet. The hero walks toward the closet to get a coat.

### Enunciado
What creates the tension here?

### Opciones
- [x] B) Dramatic Irony (We know something the character doesn't).
- [ ] A) The hero is stupid.
- [ ] C) The closet is scary.

## Question 10 (Part 10 - Connotation - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v10`

ID: UNI-ENG-12-literary-nuance-001-v10

### Contexto
**Words:** "Skinny" vs "Slender".
**Sentence:** "She had a slender figure."

### Enunciado
Why did the author choose "slender" instead of "skinny"?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) They mean exactly the same.
- [x] B) "Slender" is positive/elegant; "Skinny" can be negative/unhealthy.
- [ ] C) "Skinny" is a French word.
- [ ] D) "Slender" means fat.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** **Connotation**. Synonyms often carry different emotional weights. Slender = Elegant. Skinny = Too thin.
*Español: Connotación positiva vs negativa.*
