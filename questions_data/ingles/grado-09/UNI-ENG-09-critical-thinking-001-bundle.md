## Question 1 (Part 1 - Cause & Effect - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v1`

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v1

### Contexto
**Scenario:**
"The streets are wet, but it didn't rain."

### Enunciado
What is a logical explanation?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) The sun is shining.
- [x] B) Someone cleaned the streets with water.
- [ ] C) It is a dry season.
- [ ] D) Clouds are white.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** If "no rain" is the premise, but "wet streets" is the result, you must find an alternative cause. **Cleaning** is the only logical cause for water.
*Español: Si no llovió, la única causa lógica de calles mojadas es que alguien las lavó.*

## Question 2 (Part 2 - Fact vs Opinion - Difficulty 4)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v2`

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v2

### Enunciado
Which of the following describes a **fact**?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Blue is the most beautiful color.
- [x] B) Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
- [ ] C) Math is difficult for everyone.
- [ ] D) Pizza is delicious.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** A **fact** can be proven scientifically or observed objectively. Opinions (beautiful, difficult, delicious) are subjective.
*Español: Un hecho es comprobable. Las opiniones son subjetivas.*

## Question 3 (Part 3 - Context Clues - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v3`

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v3

### Contexto
**Sentence:**
"The *cacophony* of the city traffic gave me a headache."

### Enunciado
What does "cacophony" likely mean?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Silence
- [ ] B) Beautiful melody
- [x] C) Loud, unpleasant noise
- [ ] D) Sweet smell

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Clues: "traffic" and "gave me a headache". These imply loud/bad noise. Thus, **cacophony** means loud noise.
*Español: Pistas: tráfico y dolor de cabeza implican ruido fuerte.*

## Question 4 (Part 4 - Social Inference - Difficulty 5)

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v4

### Contexto
**Dialogue:**
Girl: "Do you really think that shirt goes with those pants?"
Boy: "Does it look bad?"

### Enunciado
What does the girl imply with her question?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) She loves his outfit.
- [ ] B) She wants to buy the shirt.
- [x] C) She thinks the clothes do not match well.
- [ ] D) She is asking about the price.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** "Do you really think..." is often a polite/indirect way of expressing doubt or criticism. She implies they **don't match**.
*Español: Es una crítica indirecta.*

## Question 7 (Part 7 - Analogy - Difficulty 5)
**ID:** `UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v7`

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v7

### Enunciado
**Bird** is to **Fly** as **Fish** is to _______?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) Walk
- [ ] B) Sky
- [x] C) Swim
- [ ] D) Water

### Explicación Pedagógica
**English:** Relationship: "Animal" + "Action/Movement". Bird flies. Fish **swims**. Water (D) is the place, not the action.
*Español: Analogía de acción. El pez nada.*

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v8

### Contexto
**Headline:**
"Lazy Teenagers Waste Time on Phones All Day."

### Enunciado
What kind of bias does this headline show?

### Opciones
- [ ] A) It is a neutral fact.
- [x] B) It is a negative generalization (stereotype).
- [ ] C) It is a positive compliment.

ID: UNI-ENG-09-critical-thinking-001-v10

Which sentence supports this claim best?

### Opciones
