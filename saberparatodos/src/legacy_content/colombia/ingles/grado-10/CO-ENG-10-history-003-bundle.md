Past Simple vs Past Perfect.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb sequence.

### Options
- [x] A) signed / had lasted
- [ ] B) had signed / lasted
- [ ] C) sign / has lasted

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-history-003-v8`

### Contexto
Past Simple vs Past Continuous vs Past Perfect.
"While the world **________** (watch) the space race, diplomats **________** (work) quietly behind the scenes because they **________** (realize) the risks."

### Enunciado
Choose the sequence for simultaneous actions and a prior realization.

### Options
- [x] A) was watching / were working / had realized
- [ ] B) watched / worked / realized
- [ ] C) has watched / work / have realized
- [ ] D) had watched / worked / realized

### Explicación Pedagógica
Use **Past Continuous** for background actions (watching/working) and **Past Perfect** for the underlying realization that preceded the actions.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-history-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"After he had finished his speech, he sat down."

### Enunciado
Is the use of Past Perfect necessary here?

### Options
- [x] A) Yes, it clarifies that the speech was completed before sitting down.
- [ ] B) No, 'After' already shows the order, so Past Simple (finished) is also acceptable and common.
- [ ] C) It is completely wrong; it should be 'has finished'.
- [ ] D) Both A and B are logically correct, but the Past Perfect emphasizes the sequence more.

### Explicación Pedagógica
While 'After' shows sequence, the **Past Perfect** is the formal way to emphasize that one action was fully completed before the next one began. Both are often accepted in modern English, but the Past Perfect is the focus here.
