### Enunciado
Choose the correct word.

### Options
- [x] A) hacker
- [ ] B) teacher
- [ ] C) doctor
- [ ] D) gardener

### Explicación Pedagógica

Second Conditional (Hypothetical Present).
"If I **________** (be) a cybersecurity expert, I would work for the government."

### Enunciado

- [ ] B) am

In Second Conditional, we use **were** for the 'if' clause (even for 'I').

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-003-v5`

### Contexto
Third Conditional (Past Regret/Alternative).
"If they **________** (install) the antivirus last week, the system wouldn't have crashed."

### Enunciado
Choose the form for a past condition that didn't happen.

### Options
- [x] A) had installed
- [ ] B) installed
- [ ] C) have installed
- [ ] D) would have installed

### Explicación Pedagógica
The Third Conditional 'if' clause uses **Past Perfect** (had + past participle).

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-003-v7`

### Contexto
Second vs Third Conditional (Meaning).
"Situation: I forgot my laptop at school yesterday.
Sentence: If I **________** (not / forget) my laptop, I would have finished the report last night."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct conditional form for this past event.

### Options
- [x] A) hadn't forgotten
- [ ] B) didn't forget
- [ ] C) wouldn't forget
- [ ] D) don't forget

### Explicación Pedagógica
Since the situation is in the past (yesterday), we use the **Third Conditional** (hadn't + past participle).

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-003-v8`

### Contexto
Mixed Conditional (Introduction/Logic).
"If I **________** (know) more about computers, I would have solved the problem yesterday."

### Enunciado
Wait, if you *still* don't know much about computers (present state) affecting a past result.
"If I **________** (be) better at technology, I wouldn't have been tricked by that phishing email."

### Options
- [x] A) were
- [ ] B) had been
- [ ] C) am
- [ ] D) will be

### Explicación Pedagógica
This is a **Mixed Conditional**: a present state (were - I am not good at tech) affecting a past result (wouldn't have been tricked).

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-003-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Cybersecurity is not just a technical issue, but a human one. If people had been educated about digital security ten years ago, many current scams would not be so successful. Experts argue that if we don't improve our digital habits now, our personal information will always be at risk. They wish everyone understood that security starts with a single click."

### Enunciado
What does the text suggest about the past?

### Options
- [x] A) That education ten years ago would have reduced the success of current scams.
- [ ] B) That scams were impossible ten years ago.
- [ ] C) That technical issues are more important than human issues.
- [ ] D) That digital habits cannot be improved now.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text uses a Third Conditional: "**If people had been educated... scams would not be so successful**" (suggesting they weren't educated enough).

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"If I would have known, I would have told you."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) We never use 'would' in the 'if' clause. It should be 'If I had known' (Past Perfect).
- [ ] B) It should be 'If I will know'.
- [ ] C) 'Told' should be 'tell'.
- [ ] D) It is correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The 'if' clause of a Third Conditional **never** takes 'would'. It uses **had** + **past participle**.
