### Options

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-002-v7`

### Contexto
Omitting the Pronoun.
"The ethical guidelines **________** the committee wrote are being ignored."

### Enunciado
In which situation can the relative pronoun be omitted?

### Options
- [x] A) When it is the object of the clause (guidelines that the committee wrote).
- [ ] B) When it is the subject of the clause.
- [ ] C) Only in non-defining clauses with commas.
- [ ] D) Never, it is always grammatically essential.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Relative pronouns can be omitted if they are the **object** of the following verb (The committee wrote [them]).

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-tech-002-v8`

### Contexto
Non-defining vs Defining.
"Ada Lovelace, **________** is considered the first programmer, was British."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct pronoun and punctuation context.

### Options
- [x] A) who (non-defining, adds extra info about a specific person).
- [ ] B) that (defining, used to identify which person).
- [ ] C) which (used for things, so incorrect here).
- [ ] D) whom (used after a preposition, so incorrect here).

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Who** is used in a non-defining clause (with commas) to add extra information about Ada Lovelace.

### Enunciado
What is the 'black box' problem according to the text?
