## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v1`

### Contexto
Vocabulary.
"The ability to find, evaluate, and use information effectively is information **________**."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct term.

### Options
- [x] A) literacy
- [ ] B) identity
- [ ] C) biology
- [ ] D) history

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Information literacy** (alfabetización informacional) is the skill set required to navigate the information age.

## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v3`

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Tense shift).
Direct: "The news is fake," the editor said.
Reported: "The editor said that the news **________** fake."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct tense for the reported statement.

### Options
- [x] A) was
- [ ] B) is
- [ ] C) were
- [ ] D) had been

### Explicación Pedagógica
In Reported Speech, we usually move the tense back one step (Present -> **Past**).

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v5`

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Questions).
Direct: "Where did you find the data?" he asked.
Reported: "He asked me **________** I had found the data."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct combination to report a question.

### Options
- [x] A) where
- [ ] B) if
- [ ] C) that
- [ ] D) did

### Explicación Pedagógica
In wh-questions, the question word (**where**) is used as a linker in reported speech.

## Question 6 (Media B - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v6`

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Punctuation/Order).
Direct: "Are you sure?" he asked.
Reported: "He asked me **________** I was sure."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct linker for a Yes/No question.

### Options
- [x] A) if (or whether)
- [ ] B) that
- [ ] C) what
- [ ] D) did

### Explicación Pedagógica
For Yes/No questions, we use "**if**" or "whether" to link the reporting verb and the clause.

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v7`

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Time references).
Direct: "The article came out yesterday," she told me.
Reported: "She told me that the article **________** the day before."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct tense shift and time reference change.

### Options
- [x] A) had come out
- [ ] B) has come out
- [ ] C) came out
- [ ] D) was come out

### Explicación Pedagógica
Past Simple changes to **Past Perfect** (had + participle) and "yesterday" becomes "the day before".

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Modals).
Direct: "You should avoid suspicious links," the IT expert said.
Reported: "The IT expert recommended that I **________** avoid suspicious links."

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-media-002-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"The 'Echo Chamber' effect occurs when users are only exposed to information that reinforces their existing beliefs. This is often caused by social media algorithms that prioritize engagement over diverse perspectives. Breaking out of an echo chamber requires intentional effort to seek out opposing viewpoints."

### Enunciado
What causes the 'Echo Chamber' effect according to the text?

### Options
- [x] A) Social media algorithms that show users what they already agree with.
- [ ] B) People intentionally seeking out all possible different opinions every day.
- [ ] C) A physical room in a library designed for studying ancient history.
- [ ] D) The lack of internet connection in mountainous rural areas of the world.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text explains it is caused by "**algorithms that prioritize engagement over diverse perspectives**".

### Contexto
Transformation / Correction.

### Enunciado

### Options

- [ ] B) hasn't known
- [ ] C) won't know
- [ ] D) wasn't know
