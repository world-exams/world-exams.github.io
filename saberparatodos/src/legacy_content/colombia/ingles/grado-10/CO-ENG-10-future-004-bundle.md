## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-future-004-v5`

### Contexto
Future Continuous vs Future Perfect Continuous.
"Don't call at 8. I **________** (drive). By 10, I **________** (drive) for two hours."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct pair.

### Options
- [x] A) will be driving / will have been driving
- [ ] B) drove / drive
- [ ] C) will drive / drove
- [ ] D) am driving / have driven

### Explicación Pedagógica
Action in progress (Future Continuous) vs Duration up to a point (Future Perfect Continuous).

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-future-004-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"By 2050, the world's population will have reached 9 billion. We will have been searching for sustainable food sources for decades. Vertical farming will become essential. While some predict resource wars, others believe technology will have solved these scarcity issues by then."

### Enunciado
What will we have been doing for decades by 2050?

### Options
- [x] A) Searching for sustainable food sources.
- [ ] B) Fighting wars.

- [ ] D) Ignoring the population.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text states: "We **will have been searching for sustainable food sources** for decades."

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-future-004-v10`

### Contexto
Sentence Order.
"been / studying / will / hours / have / three / she / for / ."

### Enunciado
Order the words to form a Future Perfect Continuous sentence.

### Options
- [x] A) She will have been studying for three hours.
- [ ] B) For three hours she will have been studying.
- [ ] C) Will have been studying she for three hours.
- [ ] D) She studying will have been for three hours.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Structure: **Subject + will have been + verb-ing + duration.**
