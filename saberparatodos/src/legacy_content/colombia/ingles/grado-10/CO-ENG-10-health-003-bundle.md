## Question 6 (Media B - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-health-003-v6`

### Contexto
Gerund vs Infinitive (Stop).
"You should stop **________** (smoke) if you want to improve your lung capacity."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct form to mean 'cease the activity'.

### Options
- [x] A) smoking
- [ ] B) to smoke
- [ ] C) smoke
- [ ] D) smoked

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Stop + gerund** means to quit an activity. (Stop + infinitive would mean to interrupt one action to do another).

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-10-health-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"I enjoy to play soccer with my friends."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) The verb 'enjoy' must be followed by a gerund (playing), not an infinitive.
- [ ] B) It should be 'I enjoying'.
- [ ] C) 'Soccer' should be 'Football'.
- [ ] D) It is correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Certain verbs like **enjoy, avoid, mind, consider** are always followed by the gerund (-ing). Correct: "I **enjoy playing**...".
