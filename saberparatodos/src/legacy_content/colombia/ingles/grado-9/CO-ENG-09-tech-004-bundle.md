## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-tech-004-v9`

Reading comprehension.
"A 'deepfake' is a video where a person's face is replaced by computer-generated imagery. It looks very realistic. Viewers might think the video is real, which can spread misinformation. If you see a politician saying something unusual in a low-quality video, it could be a fake. Always check the source before sharing."

### Enunciado
Why are deepfakes dangerous?

### Options
- [x] A) Because they can spread misinformation.
- [ ] B) Because they are low-quality.
- [ ] C) Because they are expensive to make.
- [ ] D) Because politicians like them.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text states: "Viewers might think the video is real, **which can spread misinformation**."
