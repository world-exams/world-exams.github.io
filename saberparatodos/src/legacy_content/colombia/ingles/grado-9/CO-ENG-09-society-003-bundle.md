## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-003-v3`

### Contexto
Reported Speech (Structure - Present to Past).
Direct Speech: "I love social media," said Juan.
Reported Speech: Juan said that he **________** social media.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb form for the reported statement.

### Options
- [x] A) loved
- [ ] B) loves
- [ ] C) will love
- [ ] D) is loving

### Explicación Pedagógica
In Reported Speech, if the reporting verb is in the past (said), the main verb usually moves one step back in time (**Present Simple** becomes **Past Simple**).

## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-003-v8`

### Contexto
Say vs Tell.
"She **________** me that she was happy."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct reporting verb when there is an indirect object (me).

### Options
- [x] A) told
- [ ] B) said
- [ ] C) spoke
- [ ] D) talked

### Explicación Pedagógica
We use **tell** (told) when we mention the person being spoken to. We use **say** (said) when we don't mention the person directly or use "to": "She said to me...".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-society-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
Direct: "I can swim."
Reported: "He said he can swim."

### Enunciado
What is the formal grammatical error here?

### Options
- [x] A) 'Can' should be changed to 'could' to match the past reporting verb.
- [ ] B) 'He' should be 'I'.
- [ ] C) 'Swim' should be 'swimming'.
- [ ] D) There is no error.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Formally, in Reported Speech, **can** changes to **could** when the reporting verb is in the past: "He said he **could** swim."
