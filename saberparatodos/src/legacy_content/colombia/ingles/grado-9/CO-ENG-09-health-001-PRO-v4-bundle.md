## Question 8 (Difícil B - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v8`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Gramatical + Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Analyze

### Contexto
Analyzing modal verbs in health advice.

"Students **should** exercise regularly. They **must** avoid substance abuse. They **could** try meditation for stress management."

### Enunciado
What is the difference in **strength** between the modal verbs used in these three sentences?

### Options
- [x] A) "Must" expresses obligation, "should" expresses recommendation, and "could" expresses possibility.
- [ ] B) All three modals express the same level of obligation.
- [ ] C) "Could" is the strongest, "should" is moderate, and "must" is the weakest.
- [ ] D) "Should" and "must" are interchangeable, and "could" means certainty.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Los modales tienen diferentes niveles de fuerza: **must** = obligación, **should** = recomendación, **could** = posibilidad/sugerencia. Entender esta gradación es clave para interpretar textos de salud pública.

**Bloom:** Analyze

Examining tone and purpose in a public health poster.

**Poster text:** "YOUR BODY IS NOT A GARBAGE CAN. Think before you eat! 🍎💪"

### Enunciado
What **rhetorical strategy** does this poster use to persuade the audience?

### Options

## Question 15 (Muy Difícil F - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v15`
**Type:** `single`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Read the following:

"A study published in *The Lancet* found that people who sleep fewer than 6 hours per night have a 13% higher mortality risk compared to those who sleep 7-8 hours. However, sleeping more than 9 hours was also associated with a 30% increased risk."

### Enunciado
What is the **nuanced conclusion** the reader should draw from this study?

### Options
- [x] A) Both insufficient AND excessive sleep are associated with health risks; moderation (7-8 hours) is optimal.
- [ ] B) Sleeping as much as possible is always the healthiest choice.
- [ ] C) The study proves that everyone should sleep exactly 7.5 hours.
- [ ] D) Sleep duration has no real impact on mortality according to this research.

### Explicación Pedagógica
El estudio muestra una relación en forma de **U**: tanto dormir poco (<6h) como dormir mucho (>9h) conllevan riesgos. La conclusión matizada es que la moderación (7-8h) es óptima. Evitar interpretaciones simplistas de datos es evaluación crítica de nivel alto.

## Question 16 (Análisis Profundo - Difficulty 6)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v16`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Analysis+

### Contexto
Read the following opinion article excerpt:

"The wellness industry is worth $4.5 trillion globally, yet paradoxically, mental health conditions continue to rise in developed nations. This suggests that the commercialization of health — selling supplements, fitness memberships, and mindfulness apps — may be treating symptoms rather than addressing root causes like economic inequality, social isolation, and workplace burnout. We are buying 'wellness' instead of building communities that genuinely support well-being."

### Enunciado
Select **ALL** statements that are supported by or can be logically inferred from this text. (Partial credit applies)

### Options
- [x] A) The author believes that buying health products does not solve the underlying causes of mental health problems. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The text implies a distinction between commercial "wellness" (products) and genuine "well-being" (community support). <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [ ] C) The author claims that the wellness industry has caused mental health conditions to rise.
- [ ] D) According to the text, people in developing nations have better mental health than those in developed nations.
- [x] E) The text suggests that social factors (inequality, isolation, burnout) are more important root causes than individual health choices. <!-- weight: 0.75 -->

### Scoring
- 3 correct, 0 wrong: 2.75/2.75 (100%)
- 2 correct, 0 wrong: ~70%
- Each wrong selection: -0.5

### Explicación Pedagógica
**A** es correcta: el texto dice que la industria "trata síntomas" en vez de causas raíz. **B** es correcta: el texto distingue explícitamente "wellness" (comercial) de "well-being" (genuino). **C** es incorrecta: el texto dice que hay una paradoja, no causalidad directa. **D** es incorrecta: el texto no compara con países en desarrollo. **E** es correcta con peso parcial: la mención de "root causes" implica mayor importancia, pero requiere inferencia.

## Question 17 (Evaluación Crítica - Difficulty 7)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v17`
**Type:** `multi-correct`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Lexical + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Evaluate

### Contexto
Consider this argument:

"Schools should replace physical education with mental health classes because: (1) mental health issues are increasing among teenagers, (2) students can exercise on their own time, and (3) one study showed that students who took a mindfulness class had 20% less anxiety."

### Enunciado
Select **ALL** logical weaknesses in this argument. (Partial credit applies)

### Options
- [x] A) It presents a false dilemma — schools could offer BOTH physical and mental health education rather than replacing one with the other. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The argument assumes all students have equal access to exercise opportunities outside school, which may not be true for low-income students. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] C) It relies on a single study with limited scope (20% reduction in one sample) to support a sweeping policy change. <!-- weight: 0.75 -->
- [ ] D) The argument is wrong because mental health is less important than physical health for teenagers.
- [ ] E) The argument fails because anxiety is not a real medical condition.

### Scoring
- 3 correct, 0 wrong: 2.75/2.75 (100%)
- 2 correct, 0 wrong: ~70%
- Each wrong selection: -0.5

### Explicación Pedagógica
**A** identifica una **falsa dicotomía** (logical fallacy). **B** identifica un **supuesto oculto** (hidden assumption) sobre equidad de acceso. **C** identifica **evidencia insuficiente** (un solo estudio). **D** y **E** son incorrectas: D introduce un juicio de valor no fundamentado, y E niega la realidad médica de la ansiedad. Este tipo de pregunta mide la capacidad de detectar falacias lógicas y evaluar la calidad argumentativa.

## Question 18 (Síntesis Creativa - Difficulty 8)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v18`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática + Lexical
**Bloom:** Create

### Contexto
Read both texts:

**Text A (Scientific):** "Research shows that social media use exceeding 3 hours daily is correlated with a 60% increase in depression symptoms among 14-17 year-olds. The mechanism involves social comparison, cyberbullying, and disrupted sleep patterns."

**Text B (Sociological):** "However, social media also provides crucial support networks for marginalized youth — LGBTQ+ teens, those with disabilities, and teens in rural areas — who may lack in-person community. Removing access could increase their isolation."

### Enunciado
Considering BOTH texts, which response **best synthesizes** the information into a balanced health recommendation?

### Options
- [x] A) Social media poses mental health risks when used excessively, but it also provides vital support for marginalized groups; policies should focus on reducing harmful usage patterns rather than eliminating access entirely. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The solution lies in digital literacy education that teaches healthy usage habits while preserving the community benefits for vulnerable populations. <!-- weight: 0.6 -->
- [x] C) Parents should monitor their teenagers' social media use and set time limits. <!-- weight: 0.3 -->
- [ ] D) Social media should be completely banned for all teenagers under 18 because the health risks outweigh any benefits. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A: 1.0 point (complete synthesis of both texts)
- B: 0.6 points (good solution but doesn't fully integrate Text B's specificity)
- C: 0.3 points (reasonable but simplistic, ignores Text B entirely)
- D: 0.0 points (contradicts Text B, ignores nuance)

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta requiere **síntesis**: integrar dos perspectivas aparentemente contradictorias en una conclusión equilibrada. **A** es la mejor respuesta porque reconoce tanto los riesgos (Text A) como los beneficios para grupos marginados (Text B) y propone una solución matizada. **B** es parcialmente correcta pero menos específica. **C** es simplista. **D** ignora completamente Text B.

## Question 19 (Razonamiento Abstracto - Difficulty 9)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v19`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Gramatical + Pragmática
**Bloom:** Meta-cognitive

### Contexto
Read the following paradox:

"A pharmaceutical company develops a pill that perfectly eliminates all feelings of sadness with zero side effects. Everyone who takes it reports being 'happy.' However, a philosopher argues that this creates a problem: if people can no longer experience sadness, can they truly experience happiness? And if the pill makes everyone feel identically 'happy,' has individual human experience been erased?"

### Enunciado
What is the **core philosophical tension** in this scenario, and which response addresses it most completely?

### Options
- [x] A) The tension is between eliminating suffering (utilitarian good) and preserving the full spectrum of human experience (existential necessity); true well-being may require the capacity for both joy AND sorrow. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) The paradox reveals that "happiness" is defined relationally — it requires contrast with sadness to be meaningful — so eliminating sadness may paradoxically eliminate happiness too. <!-- weight: 0.8 -->
- [x] C) The issue is about individual autonomy: if everyone feels the same, personal identity and the diversity of human experience are threatened. <!-- weight: 0.5 -->
- [ ] D) There is no real paradox — eliminating sadness is always good because sadness is always harmful to health. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A: 1.0 point (identifies both the ethical AND philosophical dimensions)
- B: 0.8 points (addresses the relational definition of happiness — strong)
- C: 0.5 points (addresses autonomy but misses the happiness-sadness dialectic)
- D: 0.0 points (misses the paradox entirely)

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta evalúa **razonamiento meta-cognitivo**: la capacidad de pensar sobre el pensamiento mismo. El estudiante debe reconocer que el concepto de "felicidad" es **relacional** (solo existe en contraste con la tristeza), y que eliminar una emoción puede destruir la otra. Este tipo de razonamiento distingue a pensadores de alto nivel cognitivo.

## Question 20 (Transferencia Extraordinaria - Difficulty 10)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-health-001-PRO-v20`
**Type:** `weighted`
**ICFES:** Comunicativa + Pragmática + Lexical + Gramatical
**Bloom:** Transfer+

### Contexto
Consider this interdisciplinary scenario:

"In ecology, a **monoculture** — growing only one crop species — makes a farm highly productive short-term but extremely vulnerable to a single disease that can destroy the entire harvest. Biodiversity provides resilience because different species respond differently to threats."

Now consider human health policy:

"Many school health programs focus exclusively on physical fitness metrics (BMI, running speed, flexibility) as the sole indicator of student health, ignoring mental health, social well-being, nutrition quality, and sleep hygiene."

### Enunciado
How does the **ecological concept of monoculture vs. biodiversity** apply as a metaphor to critique the school health program described above? Select the response that demonstrates the deepest transfer of the concept.

### Options
- [x] A) Just as agricultural monocultures are efficient but fragile, health programs that measure only physical fitness create a "monoculture of wellness" — appearing successful by one metric while remaining blind to threats from other dimensions (mental health, social well-being, nutrition), making students vulnerable to holistic health crises that a single-metric system cannot detect or prevent. <!-- weight: 1.0 -->
- [x] B) A health program needs "biodiversity" — multiple indicators — to be resilient; measuring only BMI is like planting only corn and hoping that a corn disease doesn't come. <!-- weight: 0.6 -->
- [x] C) Both monoculture farming and single-metric health programs are too narrow in their approach. <!-- weight: 0.2 -->
- [ ] D) The ecological concept is irrelevant to health policy because farms and schools are completely different systems. <!-- weight: 0.0 -->

### Scoring
- A: 1.0 point (deep transfer — maps specific ecological mechanisms to health policy with originality)
- B: 0.6 points (correct transfer with concrete example but less analytical depth)
- C: 0.2 points (recognizes the analogy at a surface level but fails to develop it)
- D: 0.0 points (rejects interdisciplinary thinking entirely)

### Explicación Pedagógica
Esta pregunta evalúa **transferencia extraordinaria**: la capacidad de tomar un concepto de un dominio (ecología) y aplicarlo creativamente a otro dominio completamente diferente (política de salud escolar). **A** demuestra la transferencia más profunda porque mapea mecanismos específicos (eficiencia vs. fragilidad, invisibilidad ante amenazas diversas) y genera una metáfora original ("monoculture of wellness"). Esta habilidad de pensamiento analógico interdisciplinario es característica de personas con alto nivel cognitivo (IQ superior) y es imposible de resolver por memorización.
