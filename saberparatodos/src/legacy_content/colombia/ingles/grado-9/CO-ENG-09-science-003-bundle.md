Vocabulary: Inventions.

### Enunciado
Choose the correct word.

### Options
- [x] A) breakthrough
- [ ] B) mistake
- [ ] C) accident
- [ ] D) holiday

### Explicación Pedagógica
A **breakthrough** (gran avance / descubrimiento) is a significant step forward in science or technology.

Grammar: Modals of Deduction (Must - certainty).
"The experiment was successful. The team **________** be very happy."

### Enunciado
Choose the modal that expresses strong certainty.

### Options
- [x] A) must
- [ ] B) shouldn't
- [ ] C) can't
- [ ] D) won't

### Explicación Pedagógica
We use **must** when we are almost certain that something is true based on results or evidence.

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-science-003-v7`

### Contexto
Modals of Deduction (Negative).
"The sample is still frozen. The freezer **________** be broken."

### Enunciado
Wait, if the sample is *still* frozen, does that mean the freezer is working or broken?
"The sample is still frozen. The freezer **________** be working correctly."

### Options
- [x] A) must
- [ ] B) can't
- [ ] C) might not
- [ ] D) shouldn't

### Explicación Pedagógica
If it's still frozen, the logical deduction is that the freezer **must** be working.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-science-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"She can't be at home. I saw her at the supermarket five minutes ago."

### Enunciado
Identify the logical function of 'can't be' in this sentence.

### Options
- [x] A) Deduction of impossibility based on evidence.
- [ ] B) Permission.
- [ ] C) General ability.
- [ ] D) Future request.

### Explicación Pedagógica
'Can't be' here is used as a **deduction of impossibility** because you have evidence (seeing her elsewhere).
