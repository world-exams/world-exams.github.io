## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v1`

### Contexto
Vocabulary: Culture.
"Beliefs and customs passed down from generation to generation are called **________**."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct noun.

### Options
- [x] A) traditions
- [ ] B) laws
- [ ] C) sports
- [ ] D) exams

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Traditions** (tradiciones) are cultural practices inherited from the past.

## Question 3 (Fácil A - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v3`

### Contexto
Used to (Past habits).
"I **________** (live) in a small village when I was a child."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct form for a past state.

### Options
- [x] A) used to live
- [ ] B) use to live
- [ ] C) am used to living
- [ ] D) usually live

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Used to + verb** describes past habits or states that are no longer true.

## Question 4 (Fácil B - Difficulty 2)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v4`

### Contexto
Used to (Negative).
"People **________** (not / have) smartphones fifty years ago."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct negative form of 'used to'.

### Options
- [x] A) didn't use to have
- [ ] B) didn't used to have
- [ ] C) not used to have
- [ ] D) don't use to have

### Explicación Pedagógica
Negative: **didn't + use + to + verb**. Note: 'use' without 'd'.

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v5`

### Contexto
Would (Past habits).
"Every summer, we **________** (visit) our grandparents at the farm."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct form for repeated past actions (nostalgia).

### Options
- [x] A) would visit
- [ ] B) will visit
- [ ] C) have visited
- [ ] D) visiting

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Would** can be used for repeated actions in the past (like 'used to'), but not for states.

## Question 6 (Media B - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v6`

### Contexto
Used to vs Would (Stative verbs).
"My grandmother **________** (love) cooking traditional meals."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct form (Love is a stative verb).

### Options
- [x] A) used to love
- [ ] B) would love
- [ ] C) is loving
- [ ] D) uses to love

### Explicación Pedagógica
With stative verbs (like love, be, know), we must use **used to**, not 'would'.

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Colombia is a country of diverse cultures. In the past, indigenous communities used to live in harmony with nature, transmitting their knowledge orally. Today, many of these traditions are disappearing. Young people would listen to stories from elders, but now technology occupies their time. Preserving our cultural identity is essential for our future."

### Enunciado
What is happening to indigenous traditions today?

### Options
- [x] A) Many of them are disappearing.
- [ ] B) They are growing stronger.
- [ ] C) They are only found on the internet.
- [ ] D) Elders don't remember them.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text states: "Today, **many of these traditions are disappearing**."

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-09-culture-004-v10`

### Contexto
Sentence Order.
"use / like / didn't / vegetable / soup / to / I / ."

### Enunciado
Order the words to form a sentence about a past dislike.

### Options
- [x] A) I didn't use to like vegetable soup.
- [ ] B) I didn't like to use vegetable soup.
- [ ] C) Vegetable soup didn't use to like I.
- [ ] D) I use to didn't like vegetable soup.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Structure: **Subject + didn't + use to + verb + object.**
