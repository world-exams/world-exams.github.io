Causative Form (Have).
"The hospital **________** (have / the equipment / repair) last week."

### Enunciado

### Options
- [x] A) had the equipment repaired
- [ ] B) had repaired the equipment
- [ ] C) have the equipment repaired

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

"Cloning raises significant bioethical questions. While therapeutic cloning could provide organs for transplant, reproductive cloning (creating a human copy) is widely banned. Critics argue it treats human life as a commodity, while supporters claim it could solve fertility issues."

### Enunciado
What is one major argument AGAINST reproductive cloning mentioned in the text?

### Options
- [x] A) It treats human life as a commodity (a product).
- [ ] B) It is too expensive for most people to afford.
- [ ] C) It solves fertility issues for many couples.
- [ ] D) It provides unlimited organs for transplants.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text mentions critics argue it "**treats human life as a commodity**".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-health-002-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"I had the barber cut my hair."

### Enunciado
How can we rephrase this using the passive causative structure (focus on the result)?

### Options
- [x] A) I had my hair cut.
- [ ] B) I had my hair cutted.
- [ ] C) I got my hair to cut.
- [ ] D) I have my hair cut.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Have + Object + Past Participle** (cut is the past participle of cut) focuses on the service done.
