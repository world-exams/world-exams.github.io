Future Perfect (Completion).

### Enunciado
Choose the correct Future Perfect form.

### Options
- [x] A) will have graduated
- [ ] B) will graduate
- [ ] C) have graduated
- [ ] D) had graduated

### Explicación Pedagógica

## Question 5 (Media A - Difficulty 3)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-work-002-v5`

### Contexto
Comparison of Future forms.
"At 5 PM tomorrow, I **________** (work), but by 8 PM, I **________** (stop)."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct combination (Continuous vs. Perfect).

### Options
- [x] A) will be working / will have stopped
- [ ] B) will have worked / will be stopping
- [ ] C) will work / will stop
- [ ] D) am working / have stopped

### Explicación Pedagógica
Future Continuous for action in progress; Future Perfect for completed action.

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

Reading comprehension.
"The gig economy is reshaping the traditional career path. Instead of a single lifelong job, workers now navigate multiple short-term contracts. By the end of the decade, it is predicted that freelancers will have become the majority of the workforce."

### Enunciado
What does the text predict about the future workforce?

### Options
- [x] A) Freelancers will likely outnumber traditional employees.
- [ ] B) Everyone will have one job for their entire life.
- [ ] C) No one will work anymore; robots will do everything.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-work-002-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"By next June, I will be graduating."

### Enunciado
How does the meaning change if we say "I will have graduated"?

### Options
- [x] A) "Will be graduating" means the event is happening then; "will have graduated" means it's already done.
- [ ] B) There is absolutely no difference in meaning.
- [ ] C) "Will have graduated" is grammatically incorrect.
- [ ] D) "Will be graduating" happened in the past.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Future Continuous emphasizes the process **during** that time; Future Perfect emphasizes **completion before** that time.
