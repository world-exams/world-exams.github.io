## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-002-v1`

### Contexto
Vocabulary.
"Presenting facts in a way that favors a particular opinion is called **________**."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct noun.

### Options
- [x] A) bias
- [ ] B) basis
- [ ] C) bass
- [ ] D) bliss

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Bias** (sesgo) occurs when information is slanted towards a specific viewpoint.

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-002-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Clickbait refers to headline text or thumbnails that are designed to attract attention and entice users to click on a link, often by being misleading or sensationalist. While effective for generating ad revenue, clickbait erodes trust in media institutions and contributes to the spread of low-quality information."

### Enunciado
What is a negative consequence of 'clickbait' mentioned in the text?

### Options
- [x] A) It erodes trust in media institutions.
- [ ] B) It helps users find high-quality information quickly.
- [ ] C) It decreases the ad revenue for websites.
- [ ] D) It makes headlines more accurate and less sensational.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text states it "**erodes trust in media institutions**".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-002-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"She recommended to read the report."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect?

### Options
- [x] A) 'Recommend' is followed by a gerund (-ing) or a 'that' clause, not an infinitive.
- [ ] B) It should be 'recommended for read'.
- [ ] C) 'Report' is a verb, not a noun here.
- [ ] D) It is actually correct.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Correct forms: "She recommended **reading**" or "She recommended **that we read**". NOT "recommended to read".
