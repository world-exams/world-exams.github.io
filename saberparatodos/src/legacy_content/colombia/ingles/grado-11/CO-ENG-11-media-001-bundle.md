## Question 1 (Muy Fácil A - Difficulty 1)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-001-v1`

### Contexto
Identifying primary digital era concepts.

### Enunciado
What does the term **'Post-Truth'** relate to or denote?

### Options
- [x] A) Circumstances in which objective facts are less influential in shaping public opinion than appeals to emotion and personal belief.
- [ ] B) A special type of document that is mailed to every citizen in the world after they finish high school.
- [ ] C) A machine that automatically deletes all the fake news from the internet every morning at 6:00 AM.
- [ ] D) A historical document that explains how to build a stone library in the 17th century.

### Explicación Pedagógica
In a **post-truth** (posverdad) era, emotional appeals often override factual evidence in public discourse.

## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-media-001-v9`

### Contexto
Analyzing a situation of "Cognitive Bias".
"A person reads a news article that supports their pre-existing political opinion and immediately shares it, but ignores a verified report that contradicts it."

### Enunciado
What specific type of cognitive bias is being demonstrated here?

### Options
- [x] A) Confirmation Bias.
- [ ] B) Sunk Cost Fallacy.
- [ ] C) Hindsight Bias.
- [ ] D) Availability Heuristic.

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Confirmation bias** (sesgo de confirmación) is the tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information in a way that confirms one's prior beliefs.
