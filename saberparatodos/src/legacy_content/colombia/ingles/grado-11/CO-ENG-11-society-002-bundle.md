## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-society-002-v9`

### Contexto
Reading comprehension.
"Cultural relativism is the principle that an individual's beliefs and activities should be understood by others in terms of that individual's own culture. It combats ethnocentrism, which is the practice of judging another culture by the standards of one's own. However, critics argue about how far this relativism should extend when human rights are violated."

### Enunciado
What is 'ethnocentrism' according to the text?

### Options
- [x] A) Judging another culture by the standards of one's own.
- [ ] B) Understanding beliefs in terms of the individual's culture.
- [ ] C) The violation of universal human rights in developing nations.
- [ ] D) The study of ancient civilizations and their rituals.

### Explicación Pedagógica
The text defines it as "**the practice of judging another culture by the standards of one's own**".

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-society-002-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"I wish I have a better understanding of the language."

### Enunciado
Why is this sentence incorrect to express a current desire?

### Options
- [x] A) "Have" should be "had" because we use Simple Past for present wishes.
- [ ] B) "Have" should be "had had" because it is a past regret.
- [ ] C) "Wish" should be "hope" if the tense is present.
- [ ] D) It is correct if you speak British English.

### Explicación Pedagógica
For present wishes (unreal situations), we shift the verb back one tense: "I wish I **had**".
