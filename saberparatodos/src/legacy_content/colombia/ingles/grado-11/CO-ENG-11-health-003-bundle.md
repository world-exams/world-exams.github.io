### Contexto
Causative HAVE (Structure).
"I **had** the doctor **________** (check) my blood pressure last week."

### Enunciado
Choose the correct verb form after 'had + person'.

### Options
- [x] A) check
- [ ] B) checked
- [ ] C) to check
- [ ] D) checking

### Explicación Pedagógica
Structure: **have + person + bare infinitive (base form)**. We ask someone to do something for us.

## Question 7 (Difícil A - Difficulty 4)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-health-003-v7`

### Contexto
Have vs Get (Nuance).
"The minister **________** the committee review the new health law." (Neutral/formal)

### Enunciado
Choose the causative verb that is more formal and direct.

### Options
- [x] A) had
- [ ] B) got
- [ ] C) made
- [ ] D) let

### Explicación Pedagógica
**Have** is more formal and neutral; **get** implies persuasion. In official contexts, **had** is preferred.

## Question 10 (Muy Difícil B - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-health-003-v10`

### Contexto
Grammar check.
"I had my car to repair at the garage."

### Enunciado
What is the error in this causative sentence?

### Options
- [x] A) With 'have + thing', we use the past participle: 'had my car repaired'.
- [ ] B) It should be 'I had my car repairing'.
- [ ] C) 'Garage' should be 'Hospital'.
- [ ] D) There is no error.

### Explicación Pedagógica
Causative **have + thing + past participle**: "I had my car **repaired**." The infinitive form ('to repair') is incorrect here.
