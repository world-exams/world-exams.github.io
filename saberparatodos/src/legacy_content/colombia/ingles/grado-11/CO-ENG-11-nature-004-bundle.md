## Question 9 (Muy Difícil A - Difficulty 5)

**ID:** `CO-ENG-11-nature-004-v9`

"Faced with rising sea levels, island nations are demanding stricter emissions targets. Arguing that they contribute the least to climate change but suffer the most, their leaders have made powerful speeches at the UN. Ignored for decades, their pleas are finally gaining traction. However, time is running out."

### Enunciado
Why are island nations demanding stricter targets?

### Options
- [x] A) Because they suffer the most from rising sea levels.

- [ ] C) Because they have too much money.
- [ ] D) Because they want to leave the UN.
