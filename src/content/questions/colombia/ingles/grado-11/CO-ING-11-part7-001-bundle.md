**The Gardener of the Andes**

The Andean bear, or Spectacled bear, is the only bear species native (1) ______ South America. It is locally known as "el oso de anteojos" because of the light-colored markings around its eyes that (2) ______ resemble glasses. These shy creatures play a crucial role in the ecosystem.

They are excellent climbers and build platforms in trees for eating and sleeping. Although they are omnivores, plants make up a large part of their diet. As they travel through the forest, they disperse seeds, (3) ______ helps to regenerate the vegetation. This is why they are often called the "gardeners of the forest".

Currently, these bears are listed as vulnerable. Their habitat is disappearing (4) ______ to deforestation and agriculture. Farmers sometimes kill them because they believe the bears attack cattle, although this (5) ______ happens. Conservation projects are now trying to educate people (6) ______ the importance of this species. Protecting the Andean bear means protecting the water sources and forests that benefit (7) ______ of us.
